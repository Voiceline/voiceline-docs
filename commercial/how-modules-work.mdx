VoiceLine is delivered in a specific sequence, for specific reasons. This page explains the model.

## Start with Field Sales Fundamentals

Every VoiceLine customer starts with [Field Sales Fundamentals (FSF)](/commercial/field-sales-fundamentals). This is the foundation — a complete package covering frontline productivity, market analytics, and sales coaching.

FSF is not a starter version. It is a complete, self-contained product that closes the full capture → analyse → coach loop for field sales teams. The majority of the value VoiceLine delivers comes from this foundation being consistently used by your team.

**No modules can be activated until FSF is live and adopted.** This is a firm requirement, not a preference.

## The first 90 days

Your contract is 12 months. The first 90 days are a dedicated opt-out window during which the focus is entirely on FSF rollout.

In this period:
- FSF is configured for your team and CRM
- Your reps are onboarded and using the product consistently
- Baseline adoption, documentation quality, and data completeness are established

Module discussions begin after day 90 — once the foundation works.

<Note>This is not a technical constraint. VoiceLine is technically capable of activating modules from day one. The 90-day FSF-only period exists because layering additional modules on top of an onboarding team destroys adoption, slows implementation quality, and delays time-to-value. We've seen this pattern repeatedly. It is enforced for your benefit.</Note>

See [Phase 1 — FSF Rollout](/implementation/our-approach/phase-1-fsf) for the full picture.

## Adding modules after day 90

Once FSF is live, modules can be added one at a time. Each module follows its own scoping, configuration, and rollout cycle.

Unlike FSF — which has a standard implementation that applies to every customer — modules often involve complex, customer-specific business logic. Pipeline management, for example, requires alignment with your sales process, opportunity stages, and CRM field structure. This takes consultation with process owners, sales executives, and end users to get right.

For this reason:
- Modules are rolled out sequentially, not in parallel
- Each module gets its own project scope and timeline
- Each module activation is a separate commercial event with its own ARR contribution

See [Phase 2 — Module Expansion](/implementation/our-approach/phase-2-expansion) for how module rollouts work in practice.

## Module pricing

Each module has a flat monthly fee per workspace — it does not scale with seat count. This keeps early adoption affordable and lets the module earn its place in your workflow before it becomes a significant line item.

As adoption grows, usage scales through [Credits](/commercial/credits) — the usage-based layer that reflects how much value your team is extracting.

See [Pricing Overview](/commercial/pricing-overview) for how all four pricing layers stack.
