Most customers get everything they need from the standard workflows and the [component library](/implementation/workflow-customisation/component-library). The component library covers the vast majority of field sales process variations across industries and CRMs.

If you have a requirement that goes beyond it, that's a signal — and we take it seriously. Most often, your SA can help you find an approach that works within the existing platform. Occasionally, it points to something genuinely worth building for all customers. Here's how that process works.

## When to raise a custom requirement

Raise a custom requirement when:

- The component library doesn't have a field type or interaction pattern your workflow needs
- Your CRM uses a non-standard object that requires a new integration mapping
- A business process you rely on has no reasonable approximation in the current standard

The first step is always a conversation with your SA. They'll assess whether an existing component covers the need, whether a minor configuration addresses it, or whether it's something new.

## How we decide what to build

We evaluate every request against two questions:

**Is the value clear?** The request should deliver measurable benefit — in adoption, data quality, or efficiency. "It matches what we had before" isn't the bar. The bar is: does this make your team's work meaningfully better?

**Does it benefit other customers?** VoiceLine is a product, not a bespoke development service. We build features that solve real problems across multiple customers — not one company's specific internal logic. When a request meets this bar, it goes on the product roadmap and ships to all customers.

If a request doesn't meet both criteria, we'll tell you directly and help you find a workable alternative.

## What typically gets built

- New field types or interaction patterns with broad applicability across field sales teams
- CRM mappings for standard objects we haven't yet supported in a given CRM
- Improvements to existing components based on real usage patterns from customers

## What we don't build

- Configurations that replicate CRM processes that weren't driving adoption — if the process wasn't working before, encoding it in VoiceLine doesn't fix it
- Highly company-specific logic that has no value to any other customer

If your process doesn't fit VoiceLine's architecture cleanly, that's often a conversation worth having. Your SA's job is to consult — to help you find the version of your process that works well within the platform. In our experience, most friction comes from overly complex processes that can be simplified without losing what matters.

## How to submit a request

Raise custom requirements through your SA or Customer Success Manager. Include:

1. **The use case** — what problem are you solving? Who experiences it, and how often?
2. **The current workaround** — what does your team do today without this capability?
3. **The expected outcome** — what changes in adoption, quality, or efficiency if this is built?
4. **The business logic** — if it requires specific rules or calculations, describe them clearly

Your SA will give you a direct answer: existing component, known roadmap item, new request under evaluation, or not a fit.

## Timelines

Requests that make the roadmap ship on product timelines. We don't provide commit dates at the time of request. Raise critical requirements early — before implementation begins — so your SA can assess feasibility within your rollout window.

## Non-standard CRM implementations

Non-standard CRM configurations (custom objects, non-standard field types, VPN-based integrations) that fall outside the component library require dedicated engineering time and are billed at **€1,300 per person day**. Your SA will scope this and give you an estimate before any work begins.
