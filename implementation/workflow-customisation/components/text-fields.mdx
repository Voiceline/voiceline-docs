Text field components capture free-form content spoken by the rep — titles, descriptions, notes, next steps, and email bodies. The same component type serves many purposes depending on the title and prompt it is configured with.

## SingleLineText

A one-line input for short, focused content. The most widely used component in the system — present in nearly every workflow.

The title and prompt determine what the AI extracts. Common uses:

| Field label | Example use |
|------------|-------------|
| Report title | A short summary of the visit, generated silently by the AI |
| Location | The city where the meeting took place |
| Main contact | The primary person spoken with |
| Company name | Which company was visited |
| Customer number | A CRM or SAP identifier |

**AI behaviour:** Most SingleLineText fields use `skip` ordering — the AI extracts the value silently from the transcript without asking. Some (like location) use `lead` to prompt the rep early in the recording if not mentioned.

**Captures:** Plain string, typically under 50 characters.

---

## MultiLineText

A multi-line input for longer content — descriptions, summaries, next steps, and anything that benefits from multiple sentences or paragraphs.

| Field label | Example use |
|------------|-------------|
| Next steps | Follow-up actions mentioned in the recording |
| Call notes | Full notes from a phone call |
| Task description | Detailed task description with all relevant context |
| Customer email | A customer-facing meeting summary |
| Business case | Justification for a deal |

**AI behaviour:** Descriptions and summaries use `skip` — the AI generates them from the full transcript. Next steps often use `trail` — the AI asks at the end "Were there any next steps discussed?" if nothing was mentioned.

**Captures:** Plain string, multi-paragraph.

---

## MarkDownText

An AI-generated email body, rendered as formatted markdown. Used exclusively in the Email workflow. The rep does not type into this field — the AI writes it.

The component reads the other email fields in the workflow (recipient, template, CC) and generates a complete email with subject line and body based on the voice recording.

**AI behaviour:** Always appears at the end of the Email workflow. Generates in the language the rep speaks — German input produces a German email.

**Captures:** Two parts — `EmailSubject` (subject line) and `MarkdownText` (email body).

**When to use:** Only in Email workflows. Not applicable to other workflow types.
