Every VoiceLine workflow is assembled from components. Each component represents a specific type of input or output: a text field, a date picker, a CRM entity selector, an AI-generated summary.

Your Solutions Architect uses this library to configure workflows to your setup. If a field or behaviour you need is listed here, it can be included in your implementation. If it is not, it can be requested through the [Product Customisation](/implementation/product-customisation/requesting-customisation) process.

## Text Inputs

### SingleLineText 1 - The Building Block

Every form needs a short text field, and SingleLineText is exactly that - a one-line input for brief, focused content. It's the most commonly used component in the system, appearing in nearly every report type.


What makes it versatile is not the component itself but the **title and prompt** it's configured with. The same component type powers radically different fields depending on context:


Report title | "Title" / "Titel" | A short summary of the visit in under 50 characters

Location | "Location" | The city where the meeting took place

Main contact | "Main Contact" / "Hauptansprechpartner" | The primary person the user spoke with

Company name | "Company" / "Unternehmen" | Which company was visited

Customer number | "Customer Number" / "Kundennummer" | An identifier like a SAP or CRM ID

ZIP code | "ZIP" | Postal code for address entry

Product interest | "Product Names" | Names of products mentioned


**Captures:** A plain string, typically under 50 characters.


**AI behavior:** Configured per instance. Some fields are `lead` (asked early in the conversation), some are `skip` (extracted silently from the transcript without asking), and some are `trail` (asked at the end). For example, the report title is usually `skip` - the AI generates it from the full transcript - while the location might be `lead`, prompting the user early if not mentioned.


**Example:** A user records a voice memo: *"I visited Siemens in Munich today and spoke with Dr. Muller about the new KR30 robot."* The AI silently extracts:


- Title: "Visit at Siemens re: KR30"

- Location: "Munich"

- Main Contact: "Dr. Muller"


All three are SingleLineText components - same type, different purpose.


---

### MultiLineText 2 - The Workhorse

When a field needs more than one line, MultiLineText takes over. It's the go-to for descriptions, summaries, next steps, comments, and any content that benefits from multiple sentences or paragraphs.


Like SingleLineText, the same component type serves many purposes through different configurations:


Task description | "Description" / "Beschreibung" | Full task details with all relevant information

Next steps | "Next Steps" / "Nächste Schritte" | Follow-up actions to take after the meeting

To-dos | "To-Dos" / "Aufgaben" | Action items extracted from the conversation

Notes | "Notes" / "Notizen" | General meeting notes

Customer email | "Customer Email" | A customer-facing summary of the interaction

Business case | "Business Case" | The business justification for a deal

Company introduction | "Company Introduction" | Background info on a company

Conclusion | "Conclusion" / "Fazit" | Final takeaways from the conversation


**Captures:** A plain string, often multi-paragraph.


**AI behavior:** For descriptions and summaries, the AI typically uses `skip` ordering - it processes the full transcript and produces structured output. For next steps, it often uses `trail` - asking the user at the end "Were there any next steps discussed?" if nothing was explicitly mentioned.


**Example:** A user dictates a five-minute voice memo about a client visit. The MultiLineText "Next Steps" component extracts: *"1. Send updated pricing proposal by Friday. 2. Schedule follow-up call with engineering team. 3. Prepare demo environment for March presentation."*


---

### MarkDownText 39 - The Email Body Generator

MarkDownText is not a text input in the traditional sense. It's a **rich content output** - typically an AI-generated email body rendered as formatted markdown. The user doesn't type into it; the system writes it.


#### How It Works

MarkDownText sits at the end of the **email composition pipeline**. It reads the other email components in the report - the recipient, the template, the CC list - and generates a complete email with subject line and body.


The generation flow:


- The user records a voice memo describing what they want to email about

- **RecipientDraftEmail** determines who it goes to (draft to self, or direct to recipient)

- **Template** selects the email tone and structure

- **CCEmail** / **BoostedCCEmail** identifies CC recipients

- **MarkDownText** takes all of this context and generates the email


**Captures:** A markdown-formatted string with two parts:


- `EmailSubject` - the email subject line

- `MarkdownText` - the email body content


**Behavior:**


- **Trailing** - always appears at the bottom of the report, after all other fields

- Has a `FinishedGenerating` flag that the frontend uses to show a loading state while the AI writes

- Post-generation cleanup removes placeholder text like "[Your Name]", "[Dein Name]", "NONE", or "KEINE"


**Multilingual:** If the user speaks in German, the email is written in German. If in English, in English. The system detects the language from the transcript and instructs the AI accordingly.


**Example:** User records: *"Schreib eine Email an Herrn Meier. Bedanke dich fur das Meeting und fasse zusammen dass wir die Preise nachste Woche schicken."*


MarkDownText generates:


`Subject: Vielen Dank fur das Meeting

Sehr geehrter Herr Meier,

vielen Dank fur das produktive Meeting heute. Wie besprochen,
werden wir Ihnen die aktualisierten Preise bis nachste Woche zukommen lassen.

Bei Fragen stehe ich Ihnen gerne zur Verfugung.

Mit freundlichen Grussen,
[Author Name]
`

## Date & Time

### DatePicker 3 - The Single Date Field

**Deprecated.** New reports should use Zeitraum with variant ZeitraumSingleDateAndTime (21003) or ZeitraumSingleDateOnly (21004) instead.


A standard date selector for capturing a single point in time. Like the text components, DatePicker serves many different purposes depending on its configuration:


Follow-up date | "Follow Up" / "Wiedervorlage" | "The next agreed touch-point with the customer"

Task due date | "Due Date" / "Falligkeitsdatum" | "The date by which the task should be completed"

Visit start | "Visit Start" / "Besuchsstart" | "The time when the visit started"

Visit end | "Visit End" / "Besuchsende" | "The time when the visit ended"

Visit day | "Visit Day" / "Besuchstag" | "The day of the visit"

Recall date | "Recall Date" / "Ruckrufdatum" | "When to call the customer back"

Delivery date | "Expected Delivery Date" | "The expected delivery date for the order"

Opportunity end date | "End Date" / "Enddatum" | "The expected close date for the opportunity"


**Captures:** A date (and optionally time) value, formatted as ISO 8601.


**AI behavior:** The AI extracts dates from natural language in the transcript. "Next Thursday", "in two weeks", "by the end of March" are all resolved to concrete dates. If the transcript doesn't mention a date, some configurations (like opportunity end date) apply defaults - e.g. 90 days from today.


**Ordering:** Follow-up and due dates are typically `trail` (asked at the end), while visit start/end times are `lead` (resolved early).


---

### Zeitraum 21 - The Time Range

Where DatePicker captures a single moment, Zeitraum (German for "time period") captures a **range** - a start and end time. It's used whenever the system needs to know *how long* something lasted.


Meeting date range | "Meeting Date Range" / "Besprechungszeitraum" | Duration of a client meeting

Call time | "Call Time" / "Anrufzeitpunkt" | When a phone call happened

Conversation time | "Call Time" / "Gesprachszeitpunkt" | Duration of a conversation

Time frame | "Time Frame" / "Zeitrahmen" | General scheduling context


**Captures:** Two ISO 8601 timestamps separated by a delimiter - start time and end time.


**AI behavior:** The AI extracts both times from the transcript. If the user says "The meeting was from 2 to 3:30", it captures `14:00|15:30`. The prompt instructs the AI to format the response as `<StartTime>|<EndTime>|`.


**Example:** User says: *"Heute Vormittag von 10 bis 11:30 Uhr war ich bei Bosch."* Zeitraum extracts: `2024-03-10T10:00:00` to `2024-03-10T11:30:00`


#### Variants

Zeitraum supports four variants that control which pickers the frontend displays:


ZeitraumDateAndTime (21001) | Date and time | Both date and time pickers. This is the default.

ZeitraumDateRangeOnly (21002) | Date range only | Date range picker with no time pickers.

ZeitraumSingleDateAndTime (21003) | Single date and time | A single date with a time picker. Replaces the deprecated DatePicker for date+time use cases.

ZeitraumSingleDateOnly (21004) | Single date only | A single date with no time picker. Replaces the deprecated DatePicker for date-only use cases.


The variant is stored in `DenseInfo.ZeitraumInfo.Variant`. The `ZeitraumInfo` struct also holds `StartDate` and `EndDate` as `time.Time` values.


**Code reference:** `library/zeitraum.go`

## Selection Inputs

### SingleSelect 4 - Pick One from a List

A dropdown where the user picks **exactly one** option from a predefined set. This is one of the most versatile components - the same type powers everything from visit reasons to opportunity stages to call directions.


The options come from **enum data** that is pre-loaded before the component renders (`NeedsData` = true). Each option has an ID, a display name, and optional multilingual translations.


Type of visit | "Type of Visit" / "Art des Besuchs" | First visit, Follow-up, Maintenance, etc.

Call direction | "Direction" / "Richtung" | Inbound, Outbound

Visit reason | "Reason for Visit" / "Besuchsgrund" | New business, Complaint, Service, etc.

Task category | "Category" / "Kategorie" | Administrative, Sales, Support, etc.

Priority | "Priority" / "Prioritat" | High, Medium, Low

Status | "Status" | Open, In Progress, Closed

Opportunity stage | "Opportunity Stage" | Prospecting, Proposal, Negotiation, Closed Won, etc.

Probability | "Probability" / "Wahrscheinlichkeit" | 10%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 90%

Industry | "Industry" / "Branche" | Manufacturing, Retail, Healthcare, etc.

Customer type | "Customer Type" / "Kundentyp" | A-Customer, B-Customer, Prospect, etc.

Salutation | "Salutation" / "Anrede" | Herr, Frau, Dr., Prof. Dr.

Language selection | "Language" / "Sprache" | German, English, French, etc.


**Captures:** One selected option ID.


**AI behavior:** The AI reads the transcript and matches the user's words to the closest enum option. The prompt always emphasizes: *"The selection must come from the provided enumeration."* If nothing matches, the component gets a **NONE** tag (`GetsNONETag` = true), allowing an explicit "no selection" rather than a guess.


**Example:** User says: *"Das war ein Erstbesuch bei der Firma."* The AI matches this to the "First visit" option in the "Type of Visit" enum.


---

### MultiSelect 5 - Pick Several from a List

The multi-select version of SingleSelect. When the answer isn't "one of these" but "all of these that apply", MultiSelect lets the user pick multiple options simultaneously.


Products | "Products" / "Produkte" | (From CRM product catalog)

Visit reasons | "Reason for Visit" / "Grund des Besuchs" | New business, Service, Training, etc.

Sales topics | "Sales Topics" / "Verkaufsthemen" | Pricing, Product demo, Contract renewal, etc.

Workflows | "Workflows" | (Workspace-specific workflow types)

Competitors | "Competitor" / "Wettbewerber" | Named competitors from config


**Captures:** One or more selected option IDs.


**AI behavior:** The AI extracts all relevant mentions. If a user discusses three products during a visit, all three should be selected. The prompt says: *"Multiple options can be selected, and the selections must come from the provided enumeration."*


---

### SearchableSingleSelect 32 - Pick One, but Searchable

When the dropdown list is too long to scroll through (hundreds of options), SearchableSingleSelect adds a **type-to-filter** search bar. Functionally identical to SingleSelect, but with a better UX for large option sets.


**Captures:** One selected option ID.


**Common uses:** Country selectors, campaign lookups, partner types - anywhere the list has dozens or hundreds of entries.


---

### CombiSelectSingle 17 - Hierarchical Pick One

Options aren't always flat lists. Sometimes they're organized in a **tree**: categories with sub-categories, product lines with specific models, regions with sub-regions.


CombiSelectSingle presents this hierarchy and lets the user pick **one leaf value**. The parent context is preserved - so when the user picks "Widget Pro" under "Industrial Widgets", the system knows both the category and the specific selection.


**Captures:** One selected option ID with hierarchical context (parent IDs).


**Example use:** "Partner type > Sub-type", "Product category > Product model".


---

### CombiSelectMulti 18 - Hierarchical Pick Many

The multi-select version of CombiSelectSingle. Users can pick **multiple** values from the hierarchical tree.


**Captures:** Multiple selected option IDs with hierarchical context.


**Example use:** "Sales topics > Sub-topics discussed" (user might discuss multiple topics across different categories).


---

### YesNo 8 - The Binary Question

A simple yes/no toggle. No dropdown, no search - just two choices. Used for binary questions where the answer is definitively one or the other.


Task exists | "Task Exists" / "Aufgabe existiert" | Does this conversation contain a task?

Opportunity | "Opportunity" | Is there a sales opportunity here?

Result | "Result" / "Ergebnis" | Was the outcome positive?

Contract cancelled | "Existing Contract Cancelled" | Did the customer cancel?

Close report | "Close Report" | Should this report be finalized?

Status update | "Set Status to In Progress" | Mark the item as in-progress?

Newsletter | "Knoll Newspaper" / "Knoll Zeitung" | Does the customer want the newsletter?

Trade fair invite | "Trade Fair Invitation" / "Messeeinladung" | Invite the customer to the trade fair?


**Captures:** Boolean - yes or no.


**AI behavior:** The AI infers the answer from context. For "Does this conversation contain a task?", it analyzes whether the user committed to any follow-up action. If the information isn't clear from the transcript, the default is usually "No."


**Example:** User says: *"Dem Kunden die aktualisierten Preise bis Freitag schicken."* The "Task Exists" component resolves to **Yes** - there's clearly a follow-up task here.

## Numeric Inputs

### CurrencyInput 6 - Money

A numeric input with currency formatting. Used wherever the value represents money - prices, deal values, budgets.


Product price | "Price" / "Preis" | "The mentioned price for the product"

Deal value | "Volume in EUR" / "Volumen" | "The expected volume of the deal in euro"


**Captures:** A numeric value (typically in the workspace's default currency).


**AI behavior:** If no price is mentioned in the transcript, the AI responds with "NONE" rather than guessing.


---

### NumericalInput 14 - Numbers (Not Money)

A plain numeric field for quantities, counts, measurements - anything numeric that isn't currency.


Power demand | "Power demand in kWh" / "Strombedarf" | "How much electricity does the customer need?"

Expected value | "Expected Value" / "Erwarteter Wert" | "The expected value of the deal"

Quantity | "Quantity" / "Menge" | "The mentioned quantity"

Participants | "Number of Participants" / "Anzahl Teilnehmer" | "How many people attended?"

Budget | "Budget" | "The mentioned budget"


**Captures:** A numeric value. If no number is mentioned, the AI returns 0 or NONE depending on configuration.


#### Variants

NumericalInput supports seven formatting variants. Each variant carries a `NumericalInputConfig` that the frontend uses to format the input - it never interprets the variant ID directly.


NICurrencyEURV (5601) | Euro currency | EUR | 2 | - | -

NICurrencyUSDV (5602) | US Dollar currency | $ | 2 | - | -

NICurrencyGBPV (5603) | British Pound currency | GBP | 2 | - | -

NICurrencyCHFV (5604) | Swiss Franc currency | CHF | 2 | - | -

NIPercentageV (5605) | Percentage | % | 0 | 0 | 100

NIIntegerV (5606) | Integer | - | 0 | - | -

NIDecimalV (5607) | Decimal | - | 2 | - | -


The config struct sent to the frontend via `DenseInfo.NumericalInputInfo`:

```go
type NumericalInputConfig struct {
    DecimalPlaces int      `json:"decimal_places"`
    MinValue      *float64 `json:"min_value,omitempty"`
    MaxValue      *float64 `json:"max_value,omitempty"`
    Symbol        string   `json:"symbol,omitempty"`
}
```

## CRM - Accounts

### CRMAccount 30 - One Company

Select **one** company or account from the CRM. This is the most fundamental CRM component - it answers the question "which customer is this report about?"


The AI extracts company names from the transcript and searches the CRM to find matches. It uses fuzzy matching with Jaro-Winkler distance scoring to handle misspellings and abbreviations.


**Captures:** One CRM account entity (ID, name, and metadata).


**AI prompt:** *"The company that was discussed during the visit. The company must be selected from the provided list. If no suitable company is available, NONE should be selected. Only a single company can be selected."*


**Common titles:** "Company" / "Firma", "Customer" / "Kunde", "Company" / "Unternehmen"


**Example:** User says: *"Heute war ich bei Siemens."* The AI extracts "Siemens", searches the CRM, and finds "Siemens AG" as a match.


---

### CRMAccounts 28 - Multiple Companies

Sometimes a report involves **more than one company** - a trade show visit, a joint meeting, or a multi-party deal. CRMAccounts lets the user tag all of them.


The AI extracts every company name mentioned in the transcript (excluding the user's own company) and matches each against the CRM.


**Captures:** Multiple CRM account entities.


**AI prompt:** *"A list of customer accounts that participated in the visit. The selling company should not be mentioned."*


**Common titles:** "Companies Involved" / "Beteiligte Unternehmen", "Involved Parties" / "Beteiligte Parteien"


**Example:** User says: *"Am Digital Marketing Summit trafen wir uns mit HubSpot, Google und Shopify."* The AI extracts three companies and matches each in the CRM.

## CRM - Contacts

### CRMRelatedContact 20 - The Main Contact

This is the **primary person** the user interacted with - the one main contact. The contact list is **filtered by the selected account**: only people who belong to that company appear as options. This filtering is the key - it prevents the user from accidentally tagging a contact at the wrong company.


**Captures:** One CRM contact entity, filtered to the selected account.


**AI prompt:** *"The contact that was met during the visit. The contact must be selected from the provided list. If no suitable contact is available, NONE should be selected."*


**Restrictions:** Only allowed in primary action types. Forbidden in non-primary actions (`ForbiddenComponentTypesForNonPrimary` = true).


**Common titles:** "Responsible" / "Verantwortlicher", "Main Contact" / "Hauptansprechpartner"


---

### CRMRelatedContacts 13 - All Attendees

Where CRMRelatedContact captures the *one* main person, CRMRelatedContacts captures **everyone who was there**. It's the participant list - all contacts from the selected account who attended the meeting or were part of the call.


The contacts are still **filtered by account**, and the AI matches names from the transcript to the filtered list. Names might be slightly misspelled in the transcript, so the matching is fuzzy.


**Captures:** Multiple CRM contact entities, filtered to the selected account.


**AI prompt:** *"A list of the attendees that should be selected from the options. Names of attendees might be slightly misspelled in the transcript. If no attendees are mentioned, return the 'NONE' option."*


**Restrictions:** Primary action types only. Cannot appear in secondary actions (`NotAllowedInSecondary` = true).


**Common titles:** "Attendees" / "Anwesende", "Participants" / "Teilnehmer"


---

### CRMRelatedContactSub 16 - The Sub-Contact

**Deprecated.** Consider using CRMCombiRelated variants for new implementations.


A **dependent** contact selector that sits below a parent contact selection. Think of it as a secondary layer: after the main contact or participant list is filled, this component shows **additional contacts** in the context of what was already selected.


It uses a `ParentPartID` to know which component it depends on, and its options are scoped accordingly.


**Captures:** One or more CRM contact entities, dependent on parent selection.


**Behavior:** Trailing - appears after its parent component. (`IsTrailing` = true)


**Common titles:** "Contact Person" / "Kontaktperson", "Main Contact" / "Hauptansprechpartner"


---

### CRMContacts 26 - External, Unfiltered Contacts

Unlike the "Related" variants above, CRMContacts shows contacts that are **not tied to any specific account**. These are free-standing people - external advisors, independent consultants, people from partner companies, or contacts who don't belong to the primary account.


This is the right component when the question is "who else was involved?" rather than "who from *this* company was there?"


**Captures:** Multiple CRM contact entities (no account filter).


**AI prompt:** *"A list of customer contacts that participated in the visit."*


**Common titles:** "External Involved" / "Externe Beteiligte", "Participants" / "Teilnehmer"

## CRM - Leads

### CRMLeadContact 50 - One Lead or One Contact

A selector that lets the user pick **either a Lead or a Contact** - a binary choice between two entity types. This exists because some CRM systems maintain separate Lead and Contact objects, and the user needs to indicate which one they're dealing with.


**Captures:** One CRM entity (either a Lead or a Contact).


**AI prompt:** *"Lead or Contact that is related to the report. If no suitable Lead or Contact is available, NONE should be selected."*


**Common titles:** "Contact or Lead" / "Lead oder Kontakt"


---

### CRMLeadContactS 51 - Multiple Leads and/or Contacts

The multi-select version. The user can tag **multiple people** who may be any mix of Leads and Contacts - a meeting might include two existing Contacts and one new Lead.


**Captures:** Multiple CRM entities (Leads and/or Contacts).


**Common titles:** "Leads & Contacts" / "Leads & Kontakte", "Additional Participants" / "Weitere Teilnehmer"

## CRM - Opportunities

### CRMRelatedOppo 43 - One Opportunity, Hard-Filtered

Select **one opportunity** from a list that is **pre-filtered to the selected account**. The user only sees opportunities belonging to the company they've already tagged. This is strict matching - pick from what's available.


The AI checks whether the user explicitly mentions a project or opportunity. The prompt is deliberately conservative: *"Do not infer an opportunity from context - the user MUST explicitly mention the project!"* Talks about warranty or feedback on already-purchased products do NOT count as opportunities.


**Captures:** One CRM opportunity entity (account-filtered).


**AI prompt:** *"First, figure out if the user explicitly uses the word 'project' (in any language). Only one project can be selected."*


**Common titles:** "Opportunity" / "Geschaftschance", "Project" / "Projekt"


---

### CRMRelatedOppos 33 - Multiple Opportunities, Hard-Filtered

Select **multiple opportunities** from the same hard-filtered list. Same strict matching as CRMRelatedOppo, but the user can tag several deals.


The AI is even more conservative here - product names must match **exactly** against the enum options, otherwise the opportunity must not be picked. This prevents false positives where a casually mentioned product name accidentally links to the wrong deal.


**Captures:** Multiple CRM opportunity entities (account-filtered).


**AI prompt:** *"Product names must match EXACTLY, else it MUST NOT be picked. Only select NONE if no opportunity is referred to."*


**Common titles:** "Related Opportunity" / "Verknupfte Geschaftschance", "Opportunities"


---

### CRMSoftRelatedOpportunities 22 - Search-Based, Not Account-Filtered

This component takes a fundamentally different approach. Instead of showing a pre-filtered list, it **searches the voice transcript** for opportunity references and uses fuzzy matching to find them in the CRM.


The flow:


- The AI extracts metadata from the transcript: document IDs (SAP numbers, project codes), opportunity names, geographic context

- The system searches the entire CRM opportunity database

- Results are scored using Jaro-Winkler distance on title similarity, with a bonus for location matches

- High-confidence matches (score > 0.9) are pre-selected; medium matches (> 0.7) are suggested


This is the right component when opportunities span across accounts or when the account context alone isn't enough to narrow down the list.


**Captures:** Multiple CRM opportunity entities (search-matched, not account-filtered).


**Restrictions:** Primary action types only (`NotAllowedInSecondary` = true).


**Common titles:** "Opportunities"


**Key distinction:** `CRMRelatedOppos` says "show me this account's deals." `CRMSoftRelatedOpportunities` says "find deals mentioned in the transcript, wherever they live."

## CRM - Products

### CRMProduct 29 - One Product

## CRM - Composite

### CRMCombiRelated 47 - The Flexible Relationship Component

#### The Problem

In a CRM, entities are rarely standalone. An Account has Contacts. An Opportunity belongs to an Account. A Product is sold through an Opportunity. These relationships matter - and users need to capture them when logging activities.


For a while, every specific pairing got its own dedicated component:


- `CRMRelatedContacts` - hardcoded to show contacts

- `CRMRelatedOppos` - hardcoded to show opportunities

- `CRMAccounts` - hardcoded to show accounts


This works until you realize the number of possible pairings grows fast. Account + Contacts, Account + Products, Account + Contacts + Opportunities... Building a separate component for each combination is a dead end.


#### The Solution

CRMCombiRelated is a **single, composable component** that replaces the need for dozens of specialized ones. Instead of hardcoding what entities it displays, it accepts a configuration - called a **variant** - that defines:


- **A main entity type** - the primary CRM object the user selects (e.g., Account)

- **One or more sub-entity types** - related objects that depend on the main selection (e.g., Contacts, Products)


The sub-entities are **filtered by the main selection**. Pick an Account first, and the Contacts dropdown narrows to only those linked to that Account. This cascading relationship is the core value - it guides the user toward meaningful, connected data rather than letting them pick unrelated records.


#### Variants


MainContactCCR (47001) | Account | Single Contact | No | - | -

MainContactsCCR (47002) | Account | Multiple Contacts | No | - | -

MainContactCCRM (47003) | Account | Multiple Contacts | **Yes** | - | -

OppoCCRM (47004) | Account | Opportunities | **Yes** | - | -

SoftOppoCCRM (47005) | Account | Soft Opportunities | No | - | -

ProjectCCRM (47006) | Account | Projects | No | - | -

TestCCRM (47007) | Account | Soft Opportunities | No | Contacts | **Yes**

CCRRelatedContactandOppo (47008) | Account | Multiple Contacts | No | Opportunities | No


**Variant titles:**


- Main entity: "Company" / "Firma"

- Sub-entity (contact): "Contact" / "Kontakt"

- Sub-entity (contacts): "Contacts" / "Kontakte"

- Sub-entity (opportunity): "Opportunity" / "Geschaftschance"

- Sub-entity (project): "Project" / "Projekt"


**Data structure:** Each variant produces a `DenseCRMCombiRelated` object containing:


- `MainType` + `MainTagged[]` + `MainSuggested[]` - the primary entity and its selections

- `SubType` + `SecondaryTagged[]` + `SecondarySuggested[]` - the dependent entity

- Optionally `TertiaryType` + tertiary selections - a third level


#### Why It Exists

This component was born primarily out of **Tasks** - a context where there is no "main tag" (unlike, say, a visit report that is inherently tied to an account). Tasks are freeform, so the user needs a flexible way to attach CRM context. Rather than building Task-specific components, CRMCombiRelated provides a general-purpose building block that works anywhere.


#### The Bigger Picture

Think of it as the shift from fixed-function tools to composable parts. Instead of a drawer full of single-purpose wrenches, it's an adjustable wrench. The variant system defines the shape, and the component handles the rest - rendering the right selectors, enforcing the right filters, and returning structured relationship data back to the backend.

## Structured Data

### LineItem 10 - The Repeatable Form

#### The Concept

Sometimes a report isn't a flat list of fields - it's a **repeating structure**. A visit report might have multiple talking points. Each talking point has a title and a description. You can't know in advance how many there will be.


LineItem solves this by creating a **parent-child structure** - a template of fields that can be repeated as many times as needed. It's the only component in the system where `HasChildren` = true.


#### How It Works

Each LineItem instance defines a **children template** - the set of sub-components that make up one "row." The user can add multiple rows, each following the same template. Each child row is a full `FrontendPart` with its own component type, title, and data.


Talking points | "Talking Points" / "Gesprachspunkte" | Title (SingleLineText) + Description (MultiLineText)

Topics with category | "Topics" | Category (SingleSelect) + Description (MultiLineText)

Lanes (Dachser) | "Lanes" | Custom freight route fields

Agenda points (Miele) | "Agenda Points" | Type (from enum) + Description

Product talking points | "Products" | Product (SingleSelect) + Notes (MultiLineText)


**Captures:** An array of child component groups (each group = one row).


**AI prompt:** *"A list of all mentioned talking points from the report. Each talking point consists of a title and a description."* For products: *"IMPORTANT: If multiple products or topics are mentioned, create a SEPARATE talking point for EACH product."*


**Example:** User records a visit where they discussed three topics - pricing, delivery timeline, and product demo. The AI creates three LineItem rows, each with a title and description extracted from the transcript.

### DenseTable 34 - The Typed Grid

#### The Concept

DenseTable is a **table where each column has its own component type**. It combines the structure of a spreadsheet with the type safety of form components. Each cell isn't just text - it's a NumericalInput, a YesNo, or a SingleSelect, depending on the column definition.


#### How It Works

Product counts (Miele) | Product models | Quantity columns per store type

**Captures:** A grid of typed values (rows x columns), where each cell's type matches its column definition.

### ReminderDateController 44 - The Reminder Scheduler

## Deduplication

### DeduplicationInformation 38 - Catch Duplicates Before They Happen

#### The Problem

When a user creates a new lead or contact via voice, there's a real risk of creating a duplicate - "John Muller" might already exist in the CRM as "Johann Mueller." Without a check, the system would create a second record, polluting the CRM.


#### The Solution

DeduplicationInformation runs a **duplicate check** against the CRM before the record is created. It searches for similar records and categorizes the matches by confidence:


**Direct Replacements** | High | Near-certain duplicate found. The system suggests linking to the existing record.

**Potential Duplicates** | Medium | Possible match - the user must decide: create new or link to existing.

**Accounts Found** | Context | Related companies found, providing context for the decision.


The component surfaces these matches in the UI so the user can:


- **Confirm** a duplicate and link to the existing record

- **Dismiss** a false positive and proceed with creation

- **Merge** records if appropriate


**Captures:** User decisions on duplicate resolution.


**When it appears:** Automatically, during lead/contact generation workflows. Not manually placed in templates.

### IMarkdownContent 49 - The AI-Generated Briefing

IMarkdownContent is the **output** of the customer intelligence pipeline. It holds the generated markdown content - structured insights assembled from parallel AI analysis.


#### How It Works

The pipeline runs **three AI analyses in parallel**, each with a specialized focus:


- **Todo Extraction** - A task management specialist extracts action items:


- Future to-dos (upcoming commitments)

- Past to-dos that are overdue (missed deadlines)

- To-dos without deadlines


- **Demand Extraction** - A B2B sales specialist identifies business opportunities:


- Active opportunities in progress

- Expressed customer interests

- Future business needs

- *Anti-hallucination rule: "Only extract EXPLICIT opportunities, not inferred ones"*


- **Complaint Extraction** - A customer service analyst identifies issues:


- Unresolved issues

- Resolved issues (for context)

- Process complaints

- *Strict rule: "Only extract complaints that are EXPLICITLY mentioned"*


The three results are then **synthesized and deduplicated** into a single markdown document with language-specific section headers (e.g., "Kurzfassung" in German, "Summary" in English).


**Captures:** Generated markdown with intermediate results (TodoResult, DemandResult, ComplaintResult) preserved for debugging.

### PBSimple 53 - The Playbook Question

#### The Concept

Sales playbooks define a set of questions that reps should ask during customer interactions - "Did you ask about their budget?", "Did you discuss the competitive landscape?", "Was a next meeting scheduled?" PBSimple represents **one question** from such a playbook.


#### How It Works

Before a conversation, the user selects a playbook collection (a set of related questions). After recording the voice memo, the system runs a **two-pass analysis**:


**Pass 1 - Boolean Check:** For each playbook question, the AI determines: *was this topic discussed? Yes or no?*


**Pass 2 - Content Extraction:** Only for questions where the answer was "yes", the AI extracts the detailed response - what exactly did the customer say about that topic?


#### Status Tracking

Each playbook question has a `PlayHappened` status:


`NotServed` (0) | The question was never asked - the user didn't cover this topic

`Served` (1) | The topic was raised but the answer was vague or non-committal

`Passed` (2) | The topic was covered and a meaningful answer was captured


#### Playbook Collections

Questions are organized into **collections** - groups of related questions that a user can select before their conversation:


"demand" | Demand & Opportunities | "Did you ask if the customer has more need for products next quarter?"

"random" | General Questions | General customer interaction questions


**Configuration:** Each PBSimple part is defined in the library TOML with a `pb` block containing multilingual descriptions:


`[parts.990001002.pb]
description.en = "This is specifically targeted to explore opportunities for the next quarter"
description.de = "Dies zielt speziell auf die Erkundung von Moglichkeiten fur das nachste Quartal ab"
`
**Captures:** Playbook response status + extracted content.

### ConditionalSelect 54 - The Visibility Controller

#### The Concept

ConditionalSelect is a single-select dropdown whose selected value controls which **child components** are visible. It solves a common form problem: some fields only make sense when a specific option is chosen. For example, a "Loss Reason" field should only appear when the opportunity stage is set to "Closed Lost."


#### How It Works

ConditionalSelect works like a regular SingleSelect - the user picks one option from an enum. But it carries a `ChildMapping` in its `DenseInfo` that maps each option ID to a list of child part IDs that should be visible when that option is selected.


The data structure:


type ConditionalSelectInfo struct {
    // ChildMapping maps option ID to list of visible child part IDs
    // Example: {"0001": [123, 124], "0002": [123, 124, 125]}
    ChildMapping map[string][]int64 `json:"child_mapping"`
}

When the user picks option "0001", only child parts 123 and 124 are shown. When they switch to "0002", part 125 also becomes visible. The frontend reads `DenseInfo.ConditionalSelectInfo.ChildMapping` to decide which children to render.


#### AI Behavior

A single LLM call generates both the parent selection and all child values at once. The AI does not need to know about the visibility mapping - it fills in everything, and the frontend hides what is not relevant.


#### Known Instances


52001 | Opportunity type | Opportunitatstyp | Switches between contract and spot deal fields

52002 | Stage | Verkaufsphase | Controls which fields appear per sales stage

52003 | Loss Reason | Verlustgrund | Captures why a deal was lost

52004 | Type | Typ | Visit type selector (Iberia-specific)


**Captures:** One selected option ID (same as SingleSelect), plus controls child visibility.


**Code reference:** `library/conditional_select.go`, `shared-structs/action-structs/frontend-report.go`

### PregnantProduct 55 - The Order Line Builder

#### The Concept

PregnantProduct is a product selector designed for order workflows. Where BookOfMaterials pairs products with quantities, PregnantProduct goes further - each selected product expands into a full set of sub-fields: quantity, unit price, total price, delivery company, delivery conditions, ship-to party, and incoterms.


Think of it as a "pregnant" product - each product you select gives birth to a set of child fields.


#### How It Works

The component loads products from the CRM catalog. When the user selects a product, it spawns child fields defined by the variant. The child fields include:


Quantity | Quantity | Menge | How many units

Unit Price | Price/unit | Preis/Einheit | Price per unit

Price | Price | Preis | Total price

Delivery Company | Delivery Company | Lieferunternehmen | Who delivers

Delivery Conditions | Delivery Conditions | Lieferbedingungen | Terms of delivery

Ship To Party | Ship To Party | Lieferempfanger | Recipient

Incoterms | Incoterms | Incoterms | International trade terms


#### Variants


PPBasicV (5501) | Basic | true | No

PPBasicNoExclV (5502) | No exclusion | false | No

PPWithPriceV (5503) | With price | true | Yes


The `ExcludeSelectedProducts` flag controls whether a product already selected in one row should be hidden from the dropdown in other rows (preventing duplicates). Note: this flag is defined but **not yet implemented** in the frontend.


The variant config is stored in `DenseInfo.PregnantProduct`:


type DensePregnantProduct struct {
    ExcludeSelectedProducts bool    `json:"exclude_selected_products"`
    OpportunityCurrency     *string `json:"opportunity_currency"`
}

**Captures:** An array of product selections, each with its child field values.


**Code reference:** `library/pregnant_product.go`

### CRMFlex 56 - The Dynamic CRM Lookup

#### The Concept

CRMFlex is a **configurable CRM entity selector** that replaces the need for dedicated components like CRMSingleSelect or CRMMultiSelect in many cases. Instead of hardcoding which entity type to display, CRMFlex reads a runtime configuration that defines:


- **Which CRM entity types** to search (contacts, leads, accounts, opportunities, or any mix)

- **What constraint** to apply (none, related to current account, or soft-related)

- **How many** the user can pick (single or multi)


This makes it the most flexible CRM component in the system - one component type that can act as a contact picker, an account picker, an opportunity finder, or a multi-entity search depending on configuration.


#### How It Works

The configuration is stored in `DenseInfo.CRMFlexInfo`:


type CRMFlexInfo struct {
    Destinations []LookupDestination `json:"destinations"`
    Cardinality  LookupCardinality   `json:"cardinality"` // "single" or "multi"
}

type LookupDestination struct {
    Entity     crm.Destination    `json:"entity"`
    Constraint LookupConstraint   `json:"constraint"` // "none", "related", "softrelated"
}

The configuration is parsed from a synthetic `_lookup_config_` entry in the component's enum options at transformation time.


#### AI Behavior

CRMFlex uses a dynamic LLM tool called `find_task_responsible`. The tool's parameters are built at runtime based on the EOM (Entity Option Map):


- `entity_var` - an enum of available entities from the EOM

- `team_name` - a free text field for team/group name fallback


The AI picks whichever parameter applies (or neither), and the system resolves the selection via Typesense search and EOM rule evaluation.


#### Known Instances


56001 | Owner | Besitzer | Task/record owner lookup

56002 | Contact or Lead (soft-related, multi) | Kontakt oder Lead (soft-related, multi) | Multi-entity contact/lead search

56003 | Account or Opportunity (related, single) | Account oder Opportunity (related, single) | Single related account or opportunity

56004 | Mixed Destinations (multi) | Gemischte Ziele (multi) | Multi-entity mixed lookup


**Captures:** One or more CRM entities, depending on cardinality.


**Note:** CRMFlex is the recommended replacement for CRMMultiSelect (12) and CRMProducts (24), as indicated by comments in the codebase (`// use CRMFlex`).


**Code reference:** `library/crm_flex.go`, `shared-structs/action-structs/frontend-report.go`

### EscalatedEventSelection 57 - Event Picker with Per-Event Children

#### The Concept

EscalatedEventSelection extends the regular EventSelection with a key addition: each selectable CRM event can have its own **child components**. This enables "before/after" workflows - for example, comparing planned values with actual results for each event.


#### How It Works

Like EventSelection, it loads calendar events from the CRM. The difference is the `EventChildMapping` in its `DenseInfo`:


type EscalatedEventSelectionInfo struct {
    EventSelection `json:",inline"`
    // Maps CRM event ID to the index in ChildParts holding the before/after children
    EventChildMapping map[string]int `json:"event_child_mapping,omitempty"`
}

When the user selects an event, the component looks up the event's ID in `EventChildMapping` to find which set of child parts to display. This allows each event to carry unique contextual data.


**Captures:** A selected event plus event-specific child component values.


**Common titles:** "Event Selection" / "Ereignisauswahl"


**Code reference:** `library/escalated_event_selection.go`
