Accounts are the core object in the VoiceLine data model — every workflow submission is linked to an account, and account scope determines what each rep can access. This configuration must be complete before any other objects are configured. For how accounts work in VoiceLine, see [Accounts](/customer/platform/data-objects/accounts).

By the end of this page your FDE will have confirmed with your CRM admin:

- The search fields needed to reliably identify accounts (output of the identification session)
- The sync filters to apply (active-only, account type, territory scope)
- Which CRM field holds the industry value on the account object
- Whether CRM permission mirroring is required (Enterprise only)

## Account identification

Account identification is the most important configuration decision for this object. It requires a joint session between your FDE and your CRM admin, working through real examples from your live CRM data.

The core problem: searching for a common account name often returns dozens of entries. The session goal is to find the minimum set of fields that lets a rep reliably narrow those results to a single, correct account — and to ensure those fields are actually synced from the CRM.

The session should surface:

- **Duplicate volume** — how many entries a typical account name search returns, and how severe the collision problem is in your data
- **Distinguishing fields** — which additional fields (customer number, address, account type) reliably differentiate entries when names collide
- **Data quality gaps** — which fields are consistently populated across your records; sparsely populated fields are not reliable as search criteria
- **How reps actually identify accounts** — whether your team works from names, customer numbers, locations, or a combination

The output is a decision on which fields to enable for search. Those fields must then be synced from the CRM — the field list below is confirmed and finalised based on this session.

## Fields to sync

| Field | Required | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Account name | Required | Primary search field |
| Account ID | Required | CRM record linkage |
| Account status | Optional | Needed for active-only sync filter |
| Account type | Optional | Needed for type-based sync filter |
| Customer number | Optional | Enable if needed to distinguish accounts — confirm in identification session |
| Address information | Optional | Enable if needed to distinguish accounts — confirm in identification session |

Additional meta fields (e.g. account segment, customer tier, custom CRM fields) can be synced for display in Visit Briefing without being searchable. Confirm with your FDE which fields to surface.

### Industry field

Industry is an account property sourced from a CRM field on the account object. It is required for analytics segmentation in VoiceLine — see [Industry Analytics](/customer/analytics/industry-analytics).

<Note>VoiceLine supports flat industry values only. Hierarchical industry trees (e.g. "Manufacturing > Automotive > Tier 1 Supplier") are not supported.</Note>

Confirm with your CRM admin which field on the account object holds the industry value (e.g. `Industry`, `Sector`, `AccountCategory`). If the CRM stores internal codes or values that differ from the display names you want (e.g. CRM stores "MFG" → should display as "Manufacturing"), your FDE configures the mapping.

## Sync filters

Filters control which accounts are synced from the CRM into VoiceLine. Accounts excluded by a filter are not visible to any rep.

| Filter | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Active only | Exclude inactive, dormant, or churned accounts |
| By account type | Sync only customers, or include prospects |
| By status field | Use a custom CRM field to define which accounts are in scope |

Overly broad sync scope increases sync time and degrades lookup performance. Your FDE will recommend appropriate filters based on your CRM data volume.

## Permissions

<Note>CRM permission mirroring requires the Enterprise platform tier. On Professional, all synced accounts are visible to all users.</Note>

On Enterprise, VoiceLine mirrors your CRM's existing permission structure — each rep sees only the accounts they have access to in the CRM. No separate permission model is configured in VoiceLine; access is derived directly from the CRM.

Confirm with your CRM admin that permissions are correctly set in the CRM before this step — VoiceLine will reflect whatever the CRM has at sync time.
