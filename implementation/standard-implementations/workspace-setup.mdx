Workspace setup covers everything that defines how the workspace is configured before any CRM connection or workflow build begins. Without confirmed settings for workspace name, languages, users, and recording access, the FDE cannot begin configuration — this is the first step and everything downstream depends on it. Your FDE handles this with your project lead. It typically takes one working session.

By the end of this page your FDE will have confirmed with your project lead:

- The workspace name and working languages to activate
- The user list (first name, last name, email, organisational role) for all pilot or rollout participants
- The recording access level (confirm with data protection or compliance team if applicable)

## Workspace name

Choose a workspace name and confirm it with your project lead before your FDE begins configuration.

The workspace name is the identifier your team sees throughout the product — in the app header, email sender names, and CRM activity records. It should reflect how your team or deployment is identified.

Renaming a workspace after go-live is possible but requires FDE involvement.

## Language settings

Decide which working languages to activate for the workspace. Once activated, each rep selects their own language during the onboarding flow — no per-user assignment is needed.

For the full list of supported languages, see [Language Settings](/customer/platform/language-settings).

**The number of languages you can activate depends on your platform tier:**

| Platform tier | Language allowance |
|---------------|--------------------|
| [Professional](/commercial/platform/professional) | Up to 3 languages; workspace configured in German or English |
| [Enterprise](/commercial/platform/enterprise) | Unlimited languages across any supported language |

If the languages required by your team exceed what your current tier allows, you will need to upgrade before configuration begins. Confirm this with your project lead at kickoff.

## CRM translation

CRM translation is an Enterprise feature. When enabled, records are automatically translated from the rep's working language into the workspace's configured CRM language on sync — so CRM data stays in a single language regardless of what each rep records in.

Confirm with your project lead whether CRM translation is required, and which language CRM records should be written in. See [Language Settings](/customer/platform/language-settings) for full details.

## User data

Provide a user list covering everyone who will take part in the pilot or rollout. Your FDE uses this to create all user accounts before go-live.

**Required fields per user:**
- First name
- Last name
- Email address
- Organisational role

The organisational role determines what the user can see in VoiceLine — specifically whether their activity is counted in coaching metrics and whether they have access to the Analytics and Coaching platforms. See [Organisational roles](/customer/platform/workspace-admin#organisational-roles) for the full role definitions and access breakdown.

Provide the user list as a spreadsheet with one row per user. Your FDE will confirm the preferred format at kickoff.

## Recording access

Decide which recording access level the workspace should use. This controls who can open and listen to recordings submitted by other reps, and whether recordings are automatically deleted after a set period.

<Note>Recording access levels 2–5 require the Enterprise platform tier. Professional workspaces are fixed to Baseline and cannot change this setting. See [Enterprise](/commercial/platform/enterprise) if an upgrade is needed.</Note>

For the full breakdown of all five access levels, see [Recording access](/customer/platform/security#recording-access).

Confirm the required access level with your project lead and your customer's data protection or compliance team before configuration begins.
