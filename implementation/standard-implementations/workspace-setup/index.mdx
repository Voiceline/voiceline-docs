Before your workflows are configured, your SA sets up the workspace foundations: how your team logs in, how VoiceLine connects to your CRM, which languages are active, and which CRM data objects VoiceLine reads at runtime.

This step is done with your CRM administrator. Your SA comes prepared with the standard configuration as a starting point — the session is about confirming and adapting, not starting from scratch.

## What workspace setup covers

| Area | What gets configured |
|------|---------------------|
| [Admin & Security](/implementation/standard-implementations/workspace-setup/admin-security) | Authentication method, CRM connection, VPN requirements, MDM, attachment handling, sync cadence |
| [Languages & Voice Recognition](/implementation/standard-implementations/workspace-setup/languages) | Interface languages, recording language, output language, AI voice recognition settings |
| [CRM Data Objects](/implementation/standard-implementations/workspace-setup/crm-data-objects) | Which CRM objects VoiceLine reads, sync scope and filters, account assignment logic per rep |

## Who needs to be involved

- **CRM administrator** — for data object scoping, field validation, and providing integration credentials
- **IT or security lead** — for authentication method and any network or MDM requirements
- **Project lead** — for timeline and scope sign-off

Workflow owners (sales managers, team leads) are typically not needed at this stage. Their input is captured separately during workflow configuration.

## How it works

Your SA arrives with a configuration document pre-populated with standard defaults. The session works through each area above, confirms what the standard covers, and captures any adjustments needed for your environment.

Any decisions that can't be made in the session — for example, confirming SSO provider details or validating CRM field names — are logged as open items with an owner and resolved before configuration begins.

The output is a finalised configuration document that becomes the record of what was agreed and the basis for the full configuration build.
