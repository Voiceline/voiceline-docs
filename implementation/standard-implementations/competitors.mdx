By the end of this page your FDE will have confirmed:

- The full competitor list with formal names and all aliases reps use in conversation
- Whether win/loss attribution is needed
- The competitor list loaded and detection active in VoiceLine


The competitor list must be confirmed before go-live — detection only applies to recordings made after a competitor is added, so any gaps at launch mean missed data. Aliases are as important as formal names and must be collected from the team before the configuration session. For how competitor tracking works in VoiceLine, see [Competitor Analytics](/customer/analytics/competitor-analytics).

## Fields to configure

| Field | Required | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Competitor name | Yes | The primary name used in your market |
| Aliases | Strongly recommended | Abbreviations or informal names reps use in conversation |
| Competitor ID | Optional | Reference ID for CRM alignment if win/loss is tracked in CRM |

Aliases are critical. If a competitor is commonly referred to by an abbreviation or shorthand, that alias must be configured — otherwise the mention will not be detected.

## Catalogue size

Most workspaces track between 5 and 50 competitors. Including too many competitors — especially broad terms that appear in unrelated contexts — increases false positives. Focus on the competitors reps actively encounter in field conversations.

## Scoping decisions

- Initial competitor list (formal names)
- Aliases and abbreviations reps use for each competitor
- Whether win/loss attribution is needed (requires [Market Analytics Pro](/commercial/modules/market-analytics-pro) and CRM field mapping)
- Which competitors are analytics-relevant vs low-signal noise
