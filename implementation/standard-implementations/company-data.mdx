Company data gives VoiceLine knowledge of your business context — what you sell, who you compete against, and the domain-specific terms your team uses. Your FDE collects this information during the scoping session and loads it before go-live. None of this requires CRM access; your project lead provides it directly.

## Products

VoiceLine uses product data to recognise product names and references in voice recordings. When a rep mentions a product by name, VoiceLine extracts it as structured data — linking it to the correct product record and surfacing it in visit report summaries, analytics, and CRM sync.

Without product data configured, VoiceLine cannot reliably identify which products a rep is referring to during a recording. This step is required for any workspace where product references in visit reports are meaningful.

For the customer-facing description of products in VoiceLine, see [Products](/customer/platform/data-objects/products).

### How product data works

Once product data is loaded, VoiceLine uses it in two ways:

1. **Voice recognition** — Product names and synonyms are added to the AI vocabulary. This improves recognition accuracy for names that are unusual, abbreviated, or likely to be misheard.

2. **Structured extraction** — When a product name is detected in a recording, VoiceLine links the mention to the product record and extracts it as a structured field — available for CRM sync and analytics.

### How product data is provided

There are two approaches. The choice depends on how often your product catalogue changes and whether your team can maintain an API sync:

| Approach | Best for | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| API sync (real-time) | Catalogues that change regularly | Keeps VoiceLine in sync automatically; requires API integration setup |
| One-time JSON upload | Stable catalogues or initial setup | FDE imports the data during configuration; re-upload required for updates |

In both cases, product data must be provided in VoiceLine's JSON format.

<Note>For the full API specification and JSON format, see the [Product Sync API docs](https://app.getvoiceline.com/api/products/syncing/docs).</Note>

### Synonyms and aliases

Synonyms are alternative names, abbreviations, or common spoken variants for a product. They are important for recognition accuracy — reps rarely use formal product names in conversation.

Examples:
- Formal name: "Industrial Cleaning Solution 500" → Synonym: "ICS 500", "the cleaner"
- Formal name: "Premium Service Package" → Synonym: "PSP", "full service"

Include synonyms in the languages your reps record in. If reps across multiple countries record in different languages, provide synonyms for each recording language.

### Scoping decisions

Before product data is loaded, confirm the following with your project lead:

- Which sync approach to use (API sync vs one-time upload)
- Who owns the product catalogue and is responsible for keeping it current
- Which product synonyms and abbreviations reps commonly use
- Whether to include all products or a subset relevant to field sales

## Competitors

VoiceLine detects competitor mentions in voice recordings automatically. When a rep mentions a competitor during a visit or call, VoiceLine extracts the reference — capturing the competitor name, the context it was mentioned in, and the account it was mentioned at.

FSF includes built-in competitor tracking. The [Market Analytics Pro](/commercial/modules/market-analytics-pro) module extends this with sub-competitor tracking, win/loss attribution, and advanced analytics dashboards.

For the customer-facing description of competitor analytics in VoiceLine, see [Competitor Analytics](/customer/analytics/competitor-analytics).

### How competitor detection works

Competitor detection is passive — it runs on every recording automatically. Reps do not need to do anything; VoiceLine scans the full transcript for any configured competitor name or alias.

Detection is **prospective only**: recordings made after a competitor is added are analysed. Recordings made before the competitor was added are not retroactively analysed.

What triggers a detection: any match of a competitor name or alias in the transcript. VoiceLine captures the name and the surrounding sentence — not just a count, but the context.

### How competitor data is provided

Competitor data is loaded directly in workspace settings by your FDE.

| Field | Required | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Competitor name | Yes | The primary name used in your market |
| Aliases | Strongly recommended | Alternative names, abbreviations, or informal names reps use in conversation |
| Competitor ID | Optional | Reference ID for CRM alignment (if win/loss is tracked in CRM) |

**Aliases are critical.** Reps rarely use a competitor's full formal name in conversation. If a competitor is commonly referred to by an abbreviation or shorthand, that alias must be configured — otherwise the mention will not be detected.

Examples:
- "SF" or "Salesforce.com" for Salesforce
- "SAP" for SAP SE
- "the orange guys" as an informal alias for a regional competitor

### Where competitor data surfaces

| Surface | What appears |
|---------|-------------|
| Visit report | Competitor mentions highlighted in the AI summary |
| Competitor Analytics | Mention frequency by competitor, time period, and account segment |
| Visit Briefing | Prior competitor mentions at the account (if any) |

### Catalogue size

Most workspaces track between 5 and 50 competitors. Including too many competitors (especially broad terms that appear in unrelated contexts) increases false positives. Focus on the competitors reps actively encounter in field conversations.

### Scoping decisions

Before competitor data is loaded, confirm the following with your project lead:

- Initial competitor list (formal names)
- Aliases and abbreviations reps use for each competitor
- Whether win/loss attribution is needed (requires Market Analytics Pro and CRM mapping)
- Which competitors are analytics-relevant vs low-signal noise

## Custom vocabulary

VoiceLine's AI voice recognition model can be extended with domain-specific vocabulary. This improves recognition accuracy for terms the general model is unlikely to know:

- Product names with non-standard spelling or abbreviations
- CRM account names that differ from common usage (e.g. "BASF" not "B-A-S-F")
- Competitor names and product names reps use colloquially
- Internal terminology, project names, or industry jargon

Custom vocabulary is provided as a list of terms and their expected spellings. Your FDE will collect this list during the scoping session. Updates after go-live are handled via workspace settings.

**When to collect this:** Before or during the scoping session — not after go-live. Missing vocabulary means early recordings will have lower recognition accuracy for those terms.

---

**What your team needs to provide for this step:**
- Product catalogue in VoiceLine JSON format (or confirmation that API sync will be used)
- Product synonyms and abbreviations reps commonly use, per recording language
- Initial competitor list with formal names and aliases
- Custom vocabulary list (domain terms, account names, internal terminology)
