The Meeting workflow lets reps create and confirm customer meetings by voice — the meeting is created in the calendar and synced to the CRM without the rep needing to open either system.

## What it captures

| Field | Type | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Meeting title | Text | AI-generated or stated by the rep |
| Account | Lookup | The customer account the meeting is with |
| Contact | Lookup | The contact attending the meeting |
| Date and time | DateTime | When the meeting is scheduled |
| Duration | Number | Meeting length in minutes |
| Location | Text | Physical address or meeting link |
| Meeting type | Picklist | e.g. product demo, follow-up, contract review |
| Notes | Text | Pre-meeting context or agenda items |

## Calendar and CRM sync

Meetings are created in the rep's connected calendar and synced to the CRM as a meeting/event record.

| CRM | Target object | Calendar | Sync trigger |
|-----|--------------|----------|-------------|
| SAP C4C | Appointment | — | On submission |
| Salesforce | Event | Google / M365 | On submission |
| MS Dynamics | Meeting | Outlook | On submission |
| HubSpot | Meeting | Google / M365 | On submission |
| ZOHO | Event | — | On submission |
| Aurea | Meeting | — | On submission |

## Default schema

The fields listed above are the standard meeting schema. The schema is configurable using the component library: fields can be added, removed, relabelled, or made required.

To add, remove, or change components, see the [Component Library](/implementation/workflow-customisation/component-library).

## Customising the schema

- **Meeting type values** — customise picklist to match your sales stage nomenclature
- **Default duration** — set default meeting length per meeting type
- **Calendar invite** — configure whether a calendar invitation is sent to the customer contact automatically
- **Attendee logic** — configure whether additional attendees can be added (e.g. rep manager, internal team)
- **Required fields** — mark date/time as required for submission

## Hard-coded fields

Hard-coded fields are FDE-configured values stamped on every meeting record created in the CRM — regardless of what the rep enters. They do not appear in the rep's UI.

| Example use case | Hard-coded field | Value |
|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| Tag source system | Source | VoiceLine |
| Enforce meeting category | Activity type | Customer Meeting |
| Default to rep's business unit | Business unit | Field Sales |

Your FDE configures hard-coded fields during workflow setup. Define the field name, the fixed value, and the CRM target field for each one.

## FAQ

**Do customer contacts receive a calendar invitation?**
This is configurable. The standard creates the meeting in the rep's calendar and CRM. Sending a customer-facing invitation requires calendar OAuth connection — your FDE enables this during setup.

**Can we link a meeting to an opportunity?**
Meeting-to-opportunity linking is available as a configuration option and requires the [Pipeline Management](/commercial/modules/pipeline-management) module.

**How do we document what happened in the meeting afterwards?**
Use a Visit Report or Call Log after the meeting to capture the outcome, next steps, and any follow-ups.
