Every VoiceLine project is staffed with four dedicated roles. Each has a defined scope and a clear handover point — so you always know who to go to and what to expect from them.

## Solutions Architect

Your SA brings deep technical knowledge of CRM processes, field sales best practices, and VoiceLine's system architecture. Their role is to bridge two worlds: how your organisation works today, and how it will work with VoiceLine.

During the evaluation and scoping phase, your SA studies your current processes and setup in detail. They identify inconsistencies, consult on optimisations, and make sure the configuration being designed will actually deliver the outcomes your business is expecting — not just technically function. Where your current processes have gaps or inefficiencies, your SA will surface them and propose a better path forward, grounded in what works across comparable deployments.

The SA's work concludes with a clearly defined scope of work — the agreed project plan covering what gets built, what integrations are required, what success looks like, and the timeline. This document is the foundation that the PM, FDE, and CSM execute against. Your SA remains available as a point of contact, but ownership of the project transfers to the rest of the team once the scope is agreed.

## Project Manager

Once the project scope is agreed and execution begins, your Project Manager takes over the organisation and coordination. Their responsibility is to keep the project on the plan you and your SA defined. They schedule and run project meetings, track open dependencies, coordinate between your stakeholders and ours, and make sure nothing is blocked or waiting on an unclear owner.

If something is at risk of slipping, your PM surfaces it early and works to resolve it. The most common cause of timeline slippage is stakeholder availability — particularly during module scoping phases, where the right process owner needs to be in the room. Your PM's job is to anticipate this and keep things moving.

## Forward Deployed Engineer

Your FDE is the technical counterpart who does the hands-on build work. They set up your CRM integration, handle data imports, configure your workspace, and implement any customisation required by your business logic. Your FDE understands both your system setup and VoiceLine's architecture in depth. Any technical question about how your CRM and VoiceLine connect — data flows, field mapping, sync behaviour, authentication — goes to your FDE.

## Customer Success Manager

Your CSM is assigned from the start but becomes your primary contact once the rollout is underway. While the SA, PM, and FDE focus on getting VoiceLine live, your CSM focuses on the people using it. They represent the voice of the user — making sure every rep and manager in scope is actually getting value from VoiceLine, not just technically onboarded.

Your CSM monitors adoption, identifies where users are struggling, and works with your team leads to address it. Their goal is to reach a point where VoiceLine is genuinely embedded in how your team works — which is also the condition that unlocks everything that comes next.

Once the initial rollout is stable, your CSM drives the expansion conversation. They'll bring the data from your first 90 days, propose which modules or user groups are the strongest next step, and scope those projects with you. Every future expansion — whether a new module or a new user group — starts with your CSM.

## Who you need on your side

Your project team handles the VoiceLine side. For the project to run smoothly, we also need the right people engaged on your side at the right times.

| Phase | Who we need from you |
|-------|---------------------|
| Scoping | A sponsor who can make decisions about project scope and commercial terms |
| Workspace setup | An IT or CRM admin who can provide integration credentials and CRM access |
| Pilot rollout | A team lead or manager who can brief reps and collect early feedback |
| Module scoping | The process owner for the specific business area the module covers |
| Expansion | Regional or segment leads for the new user groups being onboarded |

Your SA will identify exactly who needs to be involved and when during the scoping phase.
