Most customers don't roll VoiceLine out to their entire sales organisation on day one. They start with a defined group, validate the results, and then expand. This is deliberate — and it's the approach we recommend.

## Start with a control group

Your initial rollout typically covers a subset of your sales team: a region, a team, or a specific user profile. This control group is your proof of concept.

During the 90-day FSF period, this group builds the habit, generates baseline data, and starts producing the adoption and CRM quality signals that tell you whether the implementation is working. Before you expand to more users, you want to see that the foundation is solid — that reps are documenting consistently, that managers have visibility, and that the data flowing into your CRM is accurate and complete.

Once the control group is performing well, the case for expansion is concrete. You're not asking your organisation to adopt a new tool on faith — you're pointing to a group that's already doing it successfully.

## Two types of expansion

When you're ready to grow, expansion typically takes one of two forms:

**Same user profile, new region** — You're rolling out the same VoiceLine configuration to field sales reps in a different country or territory. Your workflows, CRM integration, and business logic are already proven. The main work is language setup, regional CRM field mapping, and onboarding the new team.

**New user profile, same region** — You're bringing a different type of user onto VoiceLine — for example, Key Account Managers alongside standard field sales reps, or an inside sales team alongside a field team. These users have different processes, different documentation needs, and often different CRM objects. This requires its own consultation and configuration.

In both cases, your FDE evaluates how existing VoiceLine configuration carries over and what needs to be built, adapted, or separated.

## Every user group gets the right product experience

VoiceLine is flexible by design. For each user group, we can:

- **Enable or hide specific features** — different teams need different capabilities; there's no reason to expose workflows that aren't relevant to a group's role
- **Configure separate workflows and business logic** — if your Key Account Managers have a fundamentally different sales process than your field reps, they get a configuration built for that process
- **Set up independent workspaces** — if a user group operates with separate CRM entities, separate data models, or separate management structures, they can be fully isolated in VoiceLine while still living on the same platform

We've configured VoiceLine for everything from single-region field teams to multi-country, multi-segment organisations with entirely separate business logic per user group. Whatever your structure looks like, your FDE will find the right configuration model.

## The expansion conversation

Your Customer Success Manager will initiate the expansion conversation with you — typically around the 90-day mark, once FSF adoption is validated.

Together, you'll define:

- Which user group or region is the logical next step
- What the relevant workflows and CRM requirements are for that group
- Whether existing configuration can be reused or adapted, or whether a separate setup is needed
- The business goal for the expanded rollout and how you'll measure success

We're also happy to get into this conversation earlier. If you already know which groups you want to expand to, your CSM can start mapping out the roadmap in parallel with your initial rollout — so there's no waiting when you're ready to move.
