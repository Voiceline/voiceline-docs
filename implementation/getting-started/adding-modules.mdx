Once Field Sales Fundamentals is live and your team is using it consistently, you're ready to extend VoiceLine with additional capabilities. Each add-on module — Pipeline Management, Market Analytics Pro, Customer Data, and others — brings a dedicated use case, and each one is rolled out as its own project.

## Each module is its own rollout

Activating a module is not flipping a switch. It's a mini-implementation: scoped, consulted, configured, and rolled out with the same rigour as your initial setup.

Every module follows the same cycle:

1. **Alignment** — your Customer Success Manager and SA align with you on the goal, scope, and what success looks like for this specific module
2. **Consultation** — your SA works with the relevant stakeholders on your side (process owners, sales managers, team leads) to understand your business logic and design the right configuration
3. **Configuration** — your FDE builds the module, adapting it to your CRM setup and sales process
4. **Rollout** — users are introduced to the new capability independently of existing workflows; adoption is monitored
5. **Validation** — once adoption is confirmed and the business goal is being tracked, the module is live and the next one can begin

The consultation step is where most of the time goes. Your SA works across three groups simultaneously — sales executives, process owners, and end users — each with different priorities and constraints. Their job is to navigate the tradeoffs between those perspectives and land on a configuration that maximises business value without sacrificing usability for the people who have to use it every day.

## Modules solve different problems than FSF

FSF workflows are standardised — there's a defined implementation that works across customers and CRMs, and your FDE applies it directly.

Add-on modules are different. They touch business processes that vary significantly between organisations:

- **Pipeline Management** requires alignment on your opportunity stages, what constitutes a qualified opportunity, how pipeline health is measured, and how this maps to your CRM's data model
- **Customer Data** requires agreement on deduplication logic, which fields are authoritative, and what the master data creation process looks like for your team
- **Market Analytics Pro** requires clarity on which products, competitors, and market signals matter most for your sales strategy
- **Sales Coaching Pro** requires agreement on what "good" looks like for your reps and how playbooks should be structured

These are business process decisions, not VoiceLine configuration decisions. Getting them right requires time with the right people on your side. Your SA will consult on the design; your FDE will tell you exactly what technical information they need and who needs to be in the room.

## Every module activation is a commercial decision

Each module is its own subscription, activated when you and your CSM agree it's the right time. This is intentional.

Our philosophy is simple: you should only pay for what actually drives value for your business. Activating a module before your team is ready, or before the right stakeholders are engaged, produces a configuration built on assumptions — and you're paying for something that won't perform the way it should.

When a module is activated, it has its own business goal and success metric agreed upfront. We track progress against that goal together with you throughout the rollout — so the question of whether a module is working has a concrete answer, not just a feeling.

## What determines which module comes next

Module sequence is decided jointly with your Customer Success Manager based on:

- Where the clearest value case exists right now
- Which workflows your reps are already using consistently in FSF
- Which process owners are available and engaged for the consultation phase
- Dependencies between modules (some modules build on others)

There is no fixed order. The right next module is the one your team is most ready to adopt well, with the clearest measurable business goal behind it.

## You can plan ahead without slowing down FSF

Module discussions can happen in parallel with your FSF rollout. During your 90-day FSF period, your CSM can start scoping future modules, identify the right stakeholders, and prepare the groundwork. This means when you're ready to expand, the consultation phase can begin immediately — no standing start.
