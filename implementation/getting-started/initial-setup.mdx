Introducing VoiceLine to your organisation is a structured project, not a one-time setup. It follows a defined sequence of phases — each with clear goals, defined activities, and measurable completion criteria.

We have refined this methodology across hundreds of field sales implementations, across industries, CRM platforms, and team sizes. The sequence is consistent because consistency is what produces reliable outcomes.

## Your project team

Four dedicated roles staff every VoiceLine project: a Solutions Architect who scopes the project and owns the business requirements, a Project Manager who takes over after signature to keep the project on plan, a Forward Deployed Engineer who handles the technical build end to end, and a Customer Success Manager who drives adoption during rollout and owns all future expansion. See [Your Project Team](/implementation/getting-started/project-team) for a full breakdown of each role and what you need to have in place on your side.

## Every implementation starts with Field Sales Fundamentals

Field Sales Fundamentals (FSF) is the entry point for every VoiceLine project. It covers the core productivity layer — visit and call reporting, tasks, emails, and reminders, all synced to your CRM — and activates basic analytics and coaching visibility from day one. We also show you the full analytics platform during onboarding, so you have a clear picture of where the platform goes as you grow.

FSF is the foundation that all subsequent modules and user group expansions are built on. It needs to be stable before anything else is added.

**The staged approach:**

Each additional module or user group expansion is its own rollout phase. Starting with FSF establishes two things that every expansion depends on:

- **A stable configuration baseline.** Module configurations built on a live, validated FSF setup reflect real usage patterns — not assumptions. Changes made post-launch cost significantly more than decisions made on solid ground.
- **Adoption as a prerequisite.** Reps adopting one new workflow at a time reach consistent usage faster than teams introduced to multiple workflows simultaneously. Consistent usage is what produces the data that makes expansion valuable.

The 90-day FSF-only period is contractual. It exists because the projects that follow this structure consistently outperform those that don't.

## How the initial rollout runs

1. **Workspace configuration** — your FDE sets up the CRM integration, authentication, language settings, and workflows, based on the [standard FSF implementation](/implementation/standard-implementations). You review and confirm.
2. **Pilot rollout** — a subset of your team (typically 10–20 reps) goes live first. This is where your SA validates that the configuration matches your business requirements, catches edge cases, and collects early feedback.
3. **Full rollout** — your remaining users are onboarded. Your CSM guides the process and is available to answer rep and manager questions directly.
4. **Adoption validation** — your CSM monitors documentation rates, CRM sync quality, and coaching data. This phase isn't complete until FSF is genuinely part of how your team works — not just technically live.

## From setup to steady state

| Milestone | Typical timing |
|-----------|---------------|
| Workspace configured, CRM integrated | Weeks 1–2 |
| Pilot group live (10–20 reps) | Weeks 2–4 |
| Full team rollout | Weeks 4–8 |
| FSF embedded, baseline data established | Weeks 8–12 |
| Expansion discussions begin | Month 3+ |
| First module live | Month 4–6 |
| Additional user groups onboarded | Month 6+ |

Once FSF adoption is solid — typically around the 90-day mark — the expansion conversation begins. In parallel, your Customer Success Manager can start scoping future modules and preparing the groundwork, so nothing is delayed unnecessarily when you're ready to move.

## What "FSF live" means

Your implementation isn't complete until:

- Your reps are documenting consistently using FSF workflows
- CRM sync is confirmed working for all configured workflows
- Your team lead has visibility into activity and coaching data
- You have at least 30 days of clean baseline data

This baseline is what we use to measure the impact of everything that comes next.
