Every VoiceLine implementation follows the same sequence. This isn't a rigid process for its own sake — it's the approach we've refined across hundreds of deployments, and it consistently produces the fastest path to a team that's documenting well and a CRM that's reflecting reality.

Here's what your implementation looks like.

---

## Phase 1 — FSF goes live (weeks 1–12)

The first phase is focused on one thing: getting [Field Sales Fundamentals](/commercial/field-sales-fundamentals) live and embedded into your team's daily routine.

Your Solutions Architect leads this end to end.

**What happens:**

1. **Workspace configuration** — CRM integration, authentication, language settings, workflow setup. Your SA configures everything from the [standard FSF implementation](/implementation/standard-implementations). You review and confirm.
2. **Pilot rollout** — a subset of your team (typically 10–20 reps) goes live first. This is where we validate workflows, catch edge cases, and collect early feedback.
3. **Full rollout** — the rest of your team is onboarded. Your SA guides the process and is available to answer rep questions directly.
4. **Adoption validation** — your SA monitors documentation rates, CRM sync quality, and coaching data. If adoption isn't where it needs to be, we work on it — this phase isn't complete until FSF is actually part of how your team works.

**What "FSF live" means:**
- Your reps are documenting consistently using FSF workflows
- CRM sync is confirmed working for all configured workflows
- Your team lead has visibility into activity and coaching data
- You have at least 30 days of clean baseline data

**Why FSF comes first — and why nothing else is added in this phase:**

We could technically activate modules from day one. We don't, because it reliably leads to worse outcomes.

FSF alone is a meaningful change for most sales teams — new tool, new documentation habit, new CRM data flow. Adding module-specific workflows in parallel fragments your team's attention and slows adoption of everything. The configurations you'd make before your team is using VoiceLine consistently are based on assumptions, not real usage patterns. Reconfiguring after launch is expensive.

We've seen customers activate three modules in week two and have none of them properly embedded by month six. The same customers, with the staged approach, are fully operating on FSF plus two modules by month six.

The 90-day FSF-only window is in your contract. It's there to protect your investment.

## Phase 2 — Steady state and expansion (month 3+)

Once FSF adoption is solid, Phase 2 begins. This is the long game — and the part where VoiceLine compounds in value.

**What happens in steady state:**
- Your SA monitors data quality and works with your team on any adoption gaps
- Coaching and analytics data starts to accumulate — your managers get visibility
- The baseline data from Phase 1 becomes the benchmark for measuring improvement

**Module expansion:**

When FSF is solid and you're ready to add capability, module expansion begins. See [Expanding with Modules](/implementation/our-approach/phase-2-expansion) for how this works in detail.

The short version: modules are added one at a time, each with its own consultation and rollout cycle. The sequence is decided jointly with your Customer Success Manager based on where the clearest value case is and which process owners are ready to engage.

## Example timelines

These timelines run from **Day 0 = contract signed**.

### FSF only

| Period | Activity |
|--------|----------|
| Week 1–2 | Workspace setup, CRM integration, workflow configuration |
| Week 2–4 | Pilot rollout (10–20 users), feedback and adjustments |
| Week 4–8 | Full team rollout |
| Week 8–12 | Adoption monitoring, baseline data established |
| Month 3+ | FSF embedded, module expansion discussions begin |

### FSF + 1 module *(most common)*

| Period | Activity |
|--------|----------|
| Day 0–90 | Full FSF rollout (see above) |
| Month 3–4 | Module scoping — stakeholder consultation, requirements |
| Month 4–5 | Module configuration and user testing |
| Month 5–6 | Module rollout |
| Month 6+ | Adoption confirmed, next module discussed |

**Result: 2 capabilities live by month 6.**

### FSF + 2 modules

| Period | Activity |
|--------|----------|
| Day 0–90 | Full FSF rollout |
| Month 3–5 | Module 1: scoping → configuration → rollout |
| Month 5–7 | Module 2: scoping → configuration → rollout |
| Month 7+ | All three capabilities embedded |

**Result: 3 capabilities live by month 7.**

### FSF + 3 modules *(enterprise)*

| Period | Activity |
|--------|----------|
| Day 0–90 | Full FSF rollout |
| Month 3–5 | Module 1 — typically Pipeline Management or Customer Data |
| Month 5–7 | Module 2 — typically analytics or coaching upgrade |
| Month 7–9 | Module 3 — more specialised (e.g. Industry Fairs, Marketing Surveys) |
| Month 9–12 | Full capability embedded, Year 2 roadmap defined |

**Result: 4 capabilities live by month 9.**

---

<Note>Actual timelines depend on your team size, CRM complexity, and availability of process owners for module consultation. The most common cause of timeline slippage is stakeholder availability during module scoping — identify your process owners early.</Note>
