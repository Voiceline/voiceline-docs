Once FSF is live and your team is using it consistently, you're ready to expand. Each add-on module brings a new capability — and its own rollout cycle.

## How module rollouts work

Every module follows the same cycle:

1. **Alignment** — your Customer Success Manager and SA align with you on the goal, scope, and what "done" looks like for this module
2. **Consultation** — your SA works with the relevant stakeholders on your side (process owners, sales managers, team leads) to understand your business logic and design the right configuration
3. **Configuration** — your SA builds the module using the [component library](/implementation/workflow-customisation/component-library)
4. **Rollout** — users are introduced to the new module independently of existing workflows; adoption is monitored
5. **Validation** — once adoption is confirmed, the module is considered live and the next one can begin

This cycle takes weeks, not days. The consultation step is where most of the time goes — and it's worth taking seriously. Modules that skip proper consultation end up configured around assumptions instead of real business logic, and they don't get used.

## Why modules require deep consultation

FSF workflows are standardised — there's a defined implementation that works across customers and CRMs, and your SA applies it directly.

Add-on modules are different. They touch business-critical processes that vary significantly between organisations:

- **Pipeline Management** requires alignment on your opportunity stages, what constitutes a qualified opportunity, how pipeline health is measured, and how this maps to your CRM's data model
- **Customer Data** requires agreement on deduplication logic, which fields are authoritative, and what the master data creation process looks like for your team
- **Sales Coaching Pro** requires clarity on what "good" looks like for your reps, how playbooks should be structured, and which managers are responsible for which teams

These aren't VoiceLine questions — they're business process questions. Getting them right requires time with the right people on your side. Your SA will tell you exactly who needs to be in the room.

## What determines the order of modules

Module sequence is decided jointly with your Customer Success Manager based on:

- Where the clearest value case exists right now
- Which workflows your reps are already using consistently in FSF
- Which process owners are available and engaged for the consultation phase
- Dependencies between modules (e.g. Industry Fairs requires both Customer Data and Pipeline Management)

There is no fixed order. The right next module is the one your team is most ready to adopt well.

## Timelines

Simpler modules with minimal business logic (e.g. Marketing Surveys or Inside Sales) typically take two to three weeks from alignment to rollout. Complex modules like Pipeline Management — with custom opportunity stages, CRM field mapping, and alignment on qualification logic — can take six to eight weeks.

For typical rollout schedules across common module combinations, see the [example timelines](/implementation/our-approach/how-we-work#example-timelines) in the How We Work overview.

## Module subscriptions

Each module adds a flat monthly fee from the activation date through your contract term and into renewal. Module activations are intentional decisions — your CSM will confirm the commercial terms before configuration begins.
