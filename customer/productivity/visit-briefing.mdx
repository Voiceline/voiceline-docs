Get a summary of everything you need to know about a customer — before you walk in.

## Overview

Visit Briefing prepares you for customer visits automatically. Before each scheduled visit, the app sends you a push notification with a summary of your recent interactions with that customer: past visit reports, calls, tasks, and follow-up actions — everything VoiceLine has captured about this account.

Instead of spending 10–45 minutes manually searching through CRM, email, and notes before a visit, you get the relevant context in a single view, at the right time.

<Note>Available with **[Field Sales Fundamentals](/commercial/field-sales-fundamentals)**.</Note>

**Current scope:** The briefing combines your VoiceLine-recorded interactions with historical CRM activities (visits, calls, tasks, and emails) synced from your CRM — including activity logged before VoiceLine adoption. Order and contract data from your ERP is not yet included — see the coming-soon sections below.

## Where to find it

**Automatic delivery:** A push notification arrives before each scheduled visit. Tap it to open the customer summary.

**On demand:** Open any customer's timeline in the VoiceLine app to see their full interaction history and generate a summary.

**Settings:** Profile → Visit Prep Settings → enable automated briefings and set your preferred delivery time.

## Activity summary

The activity summary shows everything VoiceLine and your CRM have recorded about this customer, organized chronologically:

- **Recent visits** — summaries of the last visit reports for this account, including who was present, what was discussed, and what follow-up was agreed
- **Open tasks** — outstanding actions from previous visits and CRM tasks that haven't been completed yet
- **Calls logged** — call log entries for this customer
- **Meetings** — upcoming and past scheduled meetings
- **Emails** — customer-facing email activity synced from your CRM
- **Created records** — opportunities, leads, or contacts created from interactions with this account

The summary is generated by the AI from both your VoiceLine recordings and the CRM activity history — not a raw list of entries, but a structured briefing with the most relevant points surfaced. VoiceLine recordings are treated as the narrative source for what was discussed; CRM activities are treated as the authoritative record of what actually happened and what is still open.

### CRM activity inclusion

The briefing automatically pulls historical activities from your CRM alongside VoiceLine-recorded interactions. This includes:

- **Visits, calls, tasks, and emails** logged in your CRM — even for interactions that happened before your team started using VoiceLine
- **Open CRM tasks** — flagged as priorities in the todo section of the briefing
- **Stakeholders and cadence** — a CRM snapshot of who the key contacts are and how often they've been engaged

Activities that appear in both VoiceLine and your CRM (for example, a visit report submitted through VoiceLine and also synced to the CRM) are deduplicated so you don't see them twice.

This means reps who have just started using VoiceLine — or customers with limited recent VoiceLine activity — still get a useful briefing on the first visit, built from the CRM history the team already has.

### Setting up automated briefings

Go to **Profile → Visit Prep Settings** to:

- **Enable automated briefings** — toggle on to receive a push notification before each scheduled visit
- **Set delivery time** — configure how far in advance the notification is sent (options configurable per rep)

Once enabled, the briefing arrives automatically for all visits in your calendar that are linked to a CRM account VoiceLine knows about.

## Playbook coaching questions

When your manager has set up a [Playbook](/customer/coaching/playbooks) for your team, the visit briefing callback includes the playbook questions automatically. After the assistant presents the activity summary for the upcoming visit, it walks you through each coaching question one by one — giving you a chance to think through key topics before you arrive.

You don't need to configure anything. If a playbook applies to you, the questions appear in the callback conversation after the summary. If no playbook is active, the callback works exactly as before.

### How it works

1. The assistant calls you with the visit briefing summary as usual
2. After presenting the summary, it asks each playbook question individually
3. You respond to each question conversationally — the assistant waits for your answer before moving to the next
4. Once all questions are addressed, the conversation transitions to follow-up actions (creating tasks, reminders, etc.)

Your responses to the playbook questions are captured and synced to the playbook analytics — the same way they would be if you answered them during a recording in the app. Your manager can review the responses in [Playbook Analytics](/customer/coaching/playbooks/analytics).

<Note>Playbook questions in visit briefing callbacks require an active playbook with a target audience that includes you. See [Playbook Builder](/customer/coaching/playbooks/builder) for setup.</Note>

## FAQ

**How far back does the activity summary go?**
The summary covers roughly the last six months of VoiceLine and CRM activity. The AI prioritizes the most recent and most relevant entries — older entries are available by scrolling the full customer timeline.

**What if I haven't visited this customer with VoiceLine before?**
You still get a briefing. The AI uses your CRM history (past visits, calls, tasks, emails synced from the CRM) to build the summary. As your team records more interactions in VoiceLine, the briefing gets richer over time.

**Can I generate a briefing on demand without a scheduled visit?**
Yes. Open the customer timeline in the app at any time and tap the summary action to generate an on-demand briefing.

**Who receives the briefing — only the rep assigned to the account?**
The briefing goes to the rep who has the visit scheduled in their calendar. It's a personal notification, not a team broadcast.

**Does the delivery time setting apply to all visits?**
Yes, the delivery time setting applies globally to all your automated briefings. You can tap the notification to open the summary at any time after it's delivered.
