Create a service case or complaint from the field — without calling inside sales or waiting until you're back at a desk.

## Overview

A case is a service issue, complaint, or technical problem that requires back-office follow-up. Currently, field reps and service technicians report these by phone or email — inside sales is often unreachable, context gets lost, and cases get logged late with incomplete information.

The Case workflow lets you create a structured case record by voice, route it to the right team or queue, and have it appear in your CRM immediately — before you've driven away from the customer site.

Two roles interact with cases differently:

- **Field sales reps** create cases when a customer reports a service issue or complaint during a sales visit
- **Field service technicians** update and resolve cases as part of their service workflow

<Note>Will be available with the **[Service Cases](/commercial/modules#service-cases)** module.</Note>

## Planned capabilities

**For field reps (case creation):**
- Create a case record by speaking the issue, affected customer, and urgency
- Route the case to a specific inside sales rep or to a service queue
- Link the case to the visit report for full context

**For service technicians (case updates):**
- Update case status as work progresses
- Add resolution notes, spare part orders, and technical observations by voice
- Capture documentation required for compliance (regulated industries: medical devices, chemicals)
- Works offline — syncs when connectivity is restored

**Queue-based routing:**
Cases can be assigned to a team queue (a pool of inside sellers who pick up available cases) rather than a specific person — matching how most CRM service workflows operate.

## FAQ

**How is a case different from a task?**
A [Task](/customer/productivity/workflows/task) is a to-do item, typically with a named assignee. A case is a CRM service object with its own lifecycle, status tracking, and queue-based routing. Cases are used in service and compliance-heavy workflows where audit trails matter.
