VoiceLine's mobile assistant lets field reps document visits, capture context, and create CRM records — by speaking, while they're still in the field.

## Overview

Documenting a customer visit takes 15–20 minutes when done manually in a CRM. Most reps don't do it immediately — they wait until the end of the day or the end of the week, by which point details are forgotten and notes get compressed into brief, low-quality entries.

The VoiceLine assistant solves this by turning spoken debrief into structured documentation. A rep finishes a meeting, speaks naturally for a few minutes about what happened, and the assistant creates the visit report in the CRM automatically. The conversation — not the typing — is the workflow.

The same assistant handles quick voice memos between meetings, business card scanning at customer sites, and a dedicated mode for industry fairs. All of it syncs to the CRM without the rep opening a laptop.

<Note>The core assistant is available with the **[Field Sales Fundamentals](/commercial/field-sales-fundamentals)** bundle. Industry Fair Mode requires the **[Industry Fairs](/commercial/modules#industry-fairs)** module.</Note>

## Where to find it

Download the **VoiceLine app** from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android). Sign in with your workspace credentials.

The assistant is also reachable by phone — see [Call functionality](#call-functionality) for details.

## Onboarding

When you first open the VoiceLine app, a product-led onboarding flow guides you through the initial setup. One of the first steps is an **introduction call** — you call the assistant and have a short introductory conversation. This gives you hands-on experience with the core recording flow before your first real visit.

The onboarding covers: account setup, voice preference selection, and scheduling your introduction call. It takes a few minutes and is designed to get you to your first real debrief as quickly as possible.

## App functionality

### Recording a visit

After a customer meeting, open the VoiceLine app and start a recording. Speak naturally about what happened — what the customer said, what you agreed on, what the next step is. You don't need to follow a template; the assistant extracts the relevant structure from your spoken debrief.

The assistant creates a visit report in your CRM within a few minutes. You can review and edit it in the app before it's submitted.

If your manager has set up a [Playbook](/customer/coaching/playbooks/index) for your visit type, the playbook questions appear at the top of the recording screen as a reference.

### Voice memos

For quick captures that don't require a full visit report — a thought while driving, a follow-up idea after a call, a note about a product conversation — use **Voice Memo** mode. Tap the microphone, speak, and the assistant processes it and stores it. No structure required.

Voice memos are particularly useful during the 2–3 hours reps typically spend driving each day, when typing is impossible but information is still fresh.

## Call functionality

The VoiceLine assistant is also accessible by phone. Instead of opening the app, you call a dedicated number and debrief conversationally. The call is processed the same way as an in-app recording.

### Territories

For security reasons, calls to the assistant are only accepted from phone numbers with approved geographic prefixes. Currently one German number is available. If you're calling from a different territory or number prefix, contact your workspace admin.

### Credits

Phone-based assistant access uses **credits** — a pool of calling minutes purchased with your workspace bundle. When credits are available, calls connect directly to the assistant. Credits are consumed per minute of call time.

When your workspace runs out of credits, the assistant is no longer reachable by phone and callers are routed to the mailbox instead.

### Mailbox

The mailbox is a fallback for when credits are exhausted. You can still call the assistant number and leave a voice message. The mailbox processes your message and triggers the same CRM workflows as a live call — you don't lose the debrief, but the conversational assistant experience is not available until credits are topped up.

## Adding context

### Images

You can attach photos to any recording or visit report. Use this for anything visual from a customer meeting — photos of whiteboards, product samples, site conditions, or printed materials.

Currently, only **business cards** are parsed and have their data extracted automatically. Other images are stored as attachments without content extraction.

<Note>Handwritten note recognition is planned for a future release.</Note>

### Business cards

Scanning a business card automatically extracts the contact's name, company, phone number, email, and address — and creates or updates the corresponding contact record in your CRM.

**To scan a business card:**

1. Tap **Scan** in the app
2. Capture the front of the card. Optionally add the back
3. Choose **Add recording** to attach a voice note, or **Save and finish** to submit the scan alone

The assistant checks for duplicates before creating a new contact. If the company already exists in your CRM, the new contact is linked to it. If the contact already exists, their record is updated.

Business card scanning also works with **email signatures** — capture a screenshot of a signature block and the same extraction logic applies.

<Note>QR code scanning on business cards is planned for a future release.</Note>

## Fair Mode

Fair Mode is a dedicated experience for industry fairs — optimized for the speed-dating style of booth interactions, where a rep meets dozens of contacts per day and needs to capture each one in under a minute.

### Managing fairs (web app)

Fairs are set up in the web app by the person responsible for the fair — typically someone from marketing. Each fair has:

- **Name** and **date range** (start and end date)
- **Participants** — the reps who will be working the booth
- **Tracking ID** — a reference that tags all CRM entries created during this fair with the fair's origin, enabling post-fair reporting and performance analysis

To create a fair: open the web app → **Industry Fairs** → **Create New Fair**.

### Activating Fair Mode

Once a fair has been set up and you've been added as a participant, you'll see an option to switch modes in the app.

Tap the menu in the home screen → **Switch to Industry Fair mode** → select the fair. A blue banner confirms: **"You are in Industry Fair mode."** Tap **Exit** to return to normal Field Sales mode.

In Fair Mode, the primary action becomes **Scan & Record** — scan a business card and immediately record a short voice note about the interaction.

### Fair reports

When you capture an interaction in Fair Mode, it's saved as a **Fair Report** — a report type designed for fair interactions. Fair reports are linked to the active fair via the tracking ID, so all interactions can be attributed to the correct event in post-fair analysis.

Currently, one fair report template is active per workspace at a time.

### Speed review

After a fair, a **Speed Review** notification prompts you to review any open actions captured during the event. Tap the notification to open a quick-action interface for processing outstanding fair entries before returning to normal operations.

**Who can see my visit reports?**
Your manager and any users with visibility rights for your team can see your submitted visit reports. Fair reports are also visible to the fair responsible person.

**What's the difference between Fair Mode and a regular visit report?**
A regular visit report documents a scheduled customer visit in the field. A Fair Report is structured for the high-volume, brief interactions typical of industry fairs — it's linked to a specific fair event via the tracking ID and optimized for fast capture.
