Create and configure playbooks — define the questions your reps see during visits and decide which reps receive them.

## Overview

The Playbook Builder is the manager's workspace for creating question sets and controlling their distribution. Each playbook consists of one or more text questions and a target audience — the group of reps who will receive it.

The builder separates two concerns: **what to ask** (the questions) and **who to ask it** (the target audience). This lets you maintain focused, relevant playbooks rather than piling all questions into one.

<Note>The Playbook Builder is available with the **[Field Sales Fundamentals](/commercial/field-sales-fundamentals)** bundle. Multiple concurrent playbooks with advanced target audience distribution require **[Sales Coaching Pro](/commercial/modules#sales-coaching-pro)**.</Note>

## Who can access the builder

The Playbook Builder is available to users with a management-level [organisational role](/customer/platform/workspace-admin#organisational-roles) — including Sales Team Leads, Sales Executives, Project Leads, CRM Managers, and OmniSellers. Users without one of these roles will not see the builder in the sidebar and cannot access it directly.

## Where to find it

Open the web app → **Playbook** in the sidebar → **Builder**. You can also reach the builder from [Playbook Analytics](/customer/coaching/playbooks/analytics) by clicking **Manage playbooks** above the playbook selector.

## Creating a playbook

1. Click **New playbook** and give it a name (e.g., "New Product Launch Q1", "Competitive Accounts").
2. Add questions — each question is a text prompt that appears to the rep during recording. Keep questions short and open-ended; reps respond by speaking, not typing.
3. Set the **target audience** — the group of reps who should receive this playbook.
4. Save and activate the playbook. It will appear for matching reps on their next recording.

## Target audiences

A target audience defines which reps receive a playbook. You can scope a playbook to a specific team, territory, role, or other segment.

If a rep matches more than one active target audience, all applicable playbooks are combined automatically — the rep sees one merged, deduplicated list of questions.

This lets you layer playbooks: a base playbook for all reps, plus a focused one for a specific product line, plus one for a competitive territory — without creating one giant playbook that everyone sees in full.

## Writing good playbook questions

Playbook questions capture information that emerges **during** the visit — things a rep learns in the room. If a rep should already know the answer before walking in, the question doesn't belong in a playbook.

### The visit test

Before adding a question, ask: *Can the rep only answer this after being in the conversation?* If yes, it's a good fit. If the answer should come from CRM research, prior knowledge, or pre-visit prep, it belongs somewhere else.

### What playbook questions are for

- **In-visit intelligence** — pain points, feedback, and new information the customer reveals during the conversation
- **Situation checks** — changes in the customer's business, team, or priorities since the last visit
- **Agreed next steps** — actions and commitments made at the end of the visit

### What playbook questions are not for

| If you want to... | Better approach |
|---|---|
| Remind reps to research the customer before the visit | Visit briefing |
| Capture one-time account facts (decision maker, current supplier) | CRM account fields |
| Track deal timelines and qualification criteria | Opportunity record |
| Assign a visit objective or type | Visit type / purpose field |

Questions aimed at pre-visit preparation feel natural to write but arrive too late — by the time a rep sees them, the visit has already started.

### Framing questions correctly

Reps answer playbook questions verbally. Frame each question so it can only be answered from what happened in the room.

**Works well:**
- "What challenges did the customer raise about their current situation?" — captures what the customer said
- "What concrete next step did you agree, and by when?" — established at the end of the visit
- "How did the customer evaluate their current supplier compared to what we offer?" — discovered through conversation

**Avoid:**
- "What did you prepare based on the CRM history?" — the rep should know this before arriving
- "Who are the decision makers at this account?" — one-time account fact; asking it every visit becomes repetitive
- "What is the project timeline for this deal?" — update the opportunity record instead

### Practical tips

- Keep each question to one focused topic.
- Phrase questions so the rep needs to be in the visit to answer them — past tense often helps ("What did the customer say about...?").
- Aim for 2–5 questions per playbook. More questions add cognitive load during the visit.
- Questions are never mandatory — reps respond verbally and can skip any of them.

<Note>Only text-based questions are supported currently. Additional question types are planned for a future release.</Note>
