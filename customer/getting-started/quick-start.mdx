Get your first rep recording their first visit — from zero to synced CRM record in four steps.

## Overview

This guide walks workspace administrators through the minimum setup required to get a rep using VoiceLine in the field. Full workspace configuration (advanced search settings, workflow customisation, language tuning) can be done after the first rep is live.

## Before you begin

You'll need:
- Workspace admin access to VoiceLine
- CRM admin access (to create a service user or configure OAuth)
- The email address of the first rep you want to onboard

---

## Step 1 — Connect your CRM

VoiceLine needs your CRM data before reps can search accounts.

1. Go to **Workspace Settings** → **Integrations**
2. Select your CRM
3. Follow the authentication steps for your CRM type:
   - **OAuth (Salesforce, Dynamics, HubSpot, ZOHO)**: Click **Connect** and authorize via your CRM login
   - **Basic auth (SAP C4C, Aurea)**: Enter your service user URL, username, and password
4. Click **Test connection** and wait for confirmation
5. The initial sync starts automatically — accounts and contacts load into VoiceLine

<Note>If your CRM is behind a VPN or firewall, ensure VoiceLine's servers are whitelisted before testing the connection. See [Integrations](/customer/platform/integrations/index#network-requirements).</Note>

---

## Step 2 — Add your product and competitor lists

VoiceLine extracts product and competitor mentions from every recording — but only for items on your lists.

**Products:**
1. Go to **Workspace Settings** → **Product Recognition**
2. Upload your product catalog (CSV) or connect to your CRM's product catalog

**Competitors:**
1. Go to **Workspace Settings** → **Competitors**
2. Add competitor names — include common abbreviations reps use in conversation

<Note>You can add competitors and products later, but extraction only applies to recordings made after they're added.</Note>

---

## Step 3 — Invite your first rep

1. Go to **Workspace Settings** → **User Management**
2. Click **Invite member**
3. Enter the rep's email address
4. The rep receives an invitation email with a link to set their password and download the app

**Rep onboarding steps** (the rep does this):
1. Open the invitation email and set a password
2. Download the VoiceLine app (iOS or Android)
3. Log in with their email and password
4. Complete the in-app onboarding: confirm language, set up voice profile, run a test recording

---

## Step 4 — Record the first visit

After a customer visit, the rep opens VoiceLine:

1. Tap **Record** (or call the VoiceLine number if using phone mode)
2. Speak naturally: *"I just visited Müller GmbH. Met with Thomas, the procurement manager. We discussed our new Model X line, he's interested in a quote. I'll send a proposal by Friday."*
3. VoiceLine extracts: account (Müller GmbH), contact (Thomas), products (Model X), follow-up (send proposal by Friday)
4. Rep reviews the structured output and taps **Submit**
5. Visit report syncs to the CRM — typically within seconds

---

## Verify it worked

In the CRM, find the account the rep visited. You should see:
- A new appointment / event / note (depending on your CRM) with the visit report content
- Any tasks or follow-ups created during the recording

In VoiceLine (web), go to **Analytics** → **Team Activity** to see the rep's first action logged.

## FAQ

**How long does the initial CRM sync take?**
Depends on the size of your CRM. Small workspaces (< 10,000 accounts): a few minutes. Large workspaces (100,000+ accounts): up to an hour for the first full sync.

**What if the rep doesn't have a smartphone?**
Reps can use VoiceLine via phone call — they call a dedicated number and record their debrief verbally. No app download required. See [Assistant — Call Functionality](/customer/productivity/assistant).

**Can I invite multiple reps at once?**
Currently, invitations are sent one at a time from the User Management UI. For bulk onboarding, contact your VoiceLine account manager.

**What if the CRM connection fails?**
Check network access (VPN/firewall), verify service user credentials have API permissions, and re-run the connection test. See your [integration's setup guide](/customer/platform/integrations/index) for CRM-specific prerequisites.
