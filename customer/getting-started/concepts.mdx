The five concepts below appear throughout VoiceLine. Understanding them will make every other part of the documentation easier to follow.

## AI Assistant

The assistant is the voice interface reps use to document customer interactions. It is available as a mobile app (iOS and Android) and via a dedicated phone number — reps choose whichever is more convenient in the moment.

After a customer visit, a rep speaks naturally for a few minutes about what happened: what the customer said, what was agreed, what the next step is. The assistant processes the recording, extracts the relevant information, and generates a structured CRM record ready for review. The rep checks the record in the app, makes any edits, and submits. The record syncs to the CRM automatically — without the rep ever opening the CRM.

The same assistant handles:

- **Full visit reports and call logs** — documented by speaking, not typing
- **Quick voice memos** — for short captures while driving or between meetings
- **Business card scanning** — scan a card and the contact is created in the CRM with data automatically extracted
- **Industry Fair Mode** — a dedicated mode for fast-paced booth interactions at events, optimized for capturing dozens of contacts per day

The assistant also serves as a search interface — reps can look up accounts, contacts, and recent activity by speaking, without opening the CRM.

→ [Assistant](/customer/productivity/assistant)

## Workflows

A workflow is a record type the assistant can create. When a rep finishes speaking, VoiceLine determines which workflow is appropriate and fills in the fields from the spoken content. The rep reviews the pre-filled record and submits.

VoiceLine includes the following workflows:

| Workflow | What it creates |
|----------|----------------|
| Visit Report | Customer visit record synced to CRM |
| Call Log | Phone call record synced to CRM |
| Task | Action item for yourself or inside sales |
| Email | AI-drafted follow-up email, sent directly or via your email client |
| Reminder | Personal calendar reminder |
| Meeting | Meeting record + calendar invite sent to attendees |
| Opportunity | New deal or update to an existing pipeline entry |
| Lead | New prospect by voice or business card scan |
| Account | New company record with duplicate detection |
| Contact | New person record by voice or business card scan |

Each workflow maps to a specific CRM object. The exact fields captured depend on your workspace configuration — a workspace admin can customize the schema for each workflow to match your CRM setup and your team's needs.

## Integrations

VoiceLine connects to your existing CRM — it does not replace it. The integration runs in both directions:

- **Inbound**: accounts, contacts, products, and other master data sync from your CRM into VoiceLine so reps can search and reference them during visits
- **Outbound**: visit reports, call logs, tasks, opportunities, and other records created by reps sync back to the CRM automatically

Reps do not need CRM credentials or access to use VoiceLine. The integration is configured once by a workspace administrator and runs in the background from that point on.

Supported CRMs include SAP Cloud for Customer, Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics 365, Aurea CRM, ZOHO CRM, and HubSpot.

## Market Insights

Every recording a rep submits is analyzed for market signals — not just to create a CRM record, but to extract intelligence about the competitive and product landscape. This happens automatically, without reps filling in any extra fields.

VoiceLine extracts:

- **Competitor mentions** — which competitors came up, what the customer's sentiment was, and whether they're gaining or losing ground
- **Product discussions** — which of your products were mentioned, how customers responded, and where opportunities were missed
- **Industry signals** — field intelligence segmented by vertical or territory

These signals are aggregated across the entire field team and surfaced in the Analytics platform. A single rep's visit might not tell you much — but when hundreds of visits are analyzed together, patterns emerge: a competitor with a new pricing campaign, a product with unexpected pushback, an industry with a shifting buying dynamic. Managers and leadership see this in near real time, without waiting for survey results or quarterly reports.

## Playbooks

A playbook is a set of questions or topics a manager wants their reps to bring up during customer visits. When a rep starts a recording, their active playbooks appear automatically at the top of the recording screen as a lightweight guide.

Playbooks close the gap between experienced reps and new ones. Instead of hoping that a rep covers a new product launch, a competitive situation, or a key account priority, a manager can add it to a playbook and it reaches every rep in that audience the next time they open the app — without changing anything about the recording workflow.

Key things to know:

- **Non-mandatory** — questions appear as a reference only and never block a rep from submitting a visit report
- **Targeted** — different playbooks can be sent to different reps based on role, territory, or other criteria
- **Tracked** — Playbook Analytics shows managers which reps are covering their playbook questions and what responses they're capturing
- **Stacked** — if multiple playbooks apply to a rep, the questions are merged into a single deduplicated list

## How the concepts connect

Each concept feeds the next. A rep uses the **assistant** to speak after a visit. The assistant creates a **workflow** record — a visit report, opportunity, or task — with fields extracted from the recording. That record syncs to the CRM via the **integration**, and master data (accounts, contacts) flows back the other way so reps always have current information at hand.

While the workflow record is being created, the same recording is also analyzed for **market insights** — competitor mentions, product reactions, sentiment — without the rep doing anything extra. Those signals accumulate across the team and surface in the Analytics dashboards for leadership.

Before the next visit, the rep sees their active **playbook** questions on the recording screen. The playbook shapes what gets discussed. What gets discussed ends up in the recording. The recording feeds both the CRM and the analytics layer.

```
Workspace admin configures
  └── Integrations (CRM sync) + Workflow schemas + Playbooks

Rep opens the assistant
  └── Playbook questions guide the conversation
        ↓
Rep speaks after the visit
  └── Assistant extracts workflow fields → rep reviews → submits
        ↓
Workflow record syncs to CRM       Market insights extracted
(visit report, opportunity, task…) (competitors, products, sentiment)
        ↓                                    ↓
CRM updated in real time       Analytics dashboards updated same day
                                             ↓
                               Manager coaches based on insights
```
