The list of competitors VoiceLine monitors for mentions in field recordings.

## Overview

Competitors are organizations that reps encounter in the field. VoiceLine uses your competitor list to automatically extract competitive mentions from visit reports and call logs — categorizing each mention by sentiment and dimension (price, quality, service, delivery, support). The accuracy of [Competitor Analytics](/customer/analytics/competitor-analytics) depends entirely on keeping this list current.

## Where to configure it

Go to **Workspace Settings** → **Competitors**.

From here, administrators can:
- Search the competitor list by name or alias to find specific entries
- Add new competitors by name
- Edit existing competitor names
- Remove competitors no longer tracked

## Configuration

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Name** | Competitor name as it appears in field conversations |
| **Aliases** | Alternative names, abbreviations, or shorthand that reps use for this competitor |

Competitor names should reflect how reps and customers refer to them in conversation. Add aliases for common abbreviations or alternate spellings so VoiceLine can match all variations to the correct competitor entry.

<Note>Typical workspaces track 5–50 competitors. Detection applies to recordings made **after** a competitor is added — historical recordings are not re-analyzed retroactively.</Note>

## Aliases

Competitors can have aliases — alternative names or abbreviations that reps and customers use in conversation. For example, a competitor "Deutsche Telekom" commonly referred to as "Telekom" or "DT" needs those terms as aliases so VoiceLine can match them to the correct competitor entry.

Aliases can be added in two ways:

- **In the administration interface** — added or edited manually for any competitor in the list through the VoiceLine web app
- **Via the analytics dashboard** — when a competitor mention from a real conversation can't be matched automatically, an admin can link it to the correct competitor and save the term as an alias; the next time a rep uses that term, it is matched correctly

When you edit a competitor's aliases, VoiceLine automatically syncs unmatched mentions: newly added aliases cause matching unmatched mentions to be reclassified as matched, and removed aliases cause previously matched mentions to reappear as unmatched.

## Matched and unmatched mentions

Every time a rep records a visit report or call log, VoiceLine analyses the transcript and extracts competitor mentions. Each mention falls into one of two categories:

- **Matched** — the mention corresponds to a known competitor on your list. VoiceLine links it automatically and includes it in [Competitor Analytics](/customer/analytics/competitor-analytics) with full sentiment and dimension tracking.
- **Unmatched** — the mention could not be linked to any entry on your competitor list. This typically happens when reps use abbreviations, trade names, or shorthand that are not yet registered as aliases.

Unmatched mentions are not lost. They appear in the [Competitor Analytics](/customer/analytics/competitor-analytics) dashboard so admins can review them and link each one to the correct competitor. Linking an unmatched mention saves the term as an alias — all future occurrences are matched automatically, and previously recorded unmatched mentions of that term are reclassified as matched.

## Search behavior

Competitors are not directly searched by reps during recordings. The list is used exclusively for automated extraction — VoiceLine scans each recording transcript and matches mentions against the competitor list. Mentions that don't match any entry are saved as unmatched competitors.

## FAQ

**How do I know if a competitor is being detected?**
Check the [Competitor Analytics](/customer/analytics/competitor-analytics) dashboard. If a competitor you know is mentioned frequently shows zero hits, verify the name matches how reps say it in recordings.

**Can I add aliases for the same competitor?**
Yes — edit the competitor entry and add alternative names as aliases. VoiceLine matches against all aliases when extracting competitor mentions from recordings.

**Are competitor mentions extracted from all recording types?**
Yes — visit reports and call logs are both analyzed. Competitors mentioned in any field recording are captured.
