Your company's sellable items — organised in a searchable hierarchy that reps reference when logging visits, opportunities, and offers.

## Overview

Products are your company's catalogue indexed in VoiceLine. Reps reference them when logging visit reports, creating opportunities, or capturing what a customer discussed or ordered. The catalogue is structured as a hierarchy of product groups containing products, which can have variants — so reps can identify and log the exact item, configuration, or SKU discussed in a conversation.

## Product hierarchy

Products in VoiceLine are organised in three layers:

- **Product groups** — categories and sub-categories that organise your catalogue (e.g., Workwear → Trousers)
- **Products** — individual sellable items, each belonging to a product group
- **Variants** — selectable options within a product (e.g., size, colour, configuration)

Product groups can nest to any depth. Products sit at the leaves of the group tree. Variants sit under individual products.

**Example — workwear catalogue:**

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Product group | Workwear |
| Sub-group | Trousers |
| Product | Cargo Pant Pro |
| Variant | Black / Standard |
| Variant | Navy / Reinforced knees |

## Aliases

Products, groups, and variants can have aliases — alternative names or abbreviations that reps use in conversation. For example, a "Super Blaster 2000" commonly referred to as "SB2000" needs "SB2000" as an alias so VoiceLine can match what the rep says to the correct product in the catalogue.

Aliases can be added in three ways:

- **During catalogue sync** — included directly in the JSON payload alongside the product data
- **In the administration interface** — added or edited manually for any product or product group in the hierarchy through the VoiceLine web app
- **Via the performance dashboard** — when a product mention from a real conversation can't be matched automatically, an admin can link it to the correct product or group and save the term as an alias; the next time a rep uses that term, it is matched correctly

## Matched and unmatched mentions

Every time a rep records a visit report or call log, VoiceLine analyses the transcript and extracts product mentions. Each mention falls into one of two categories:

- **Matched** — the mention corresponds to a known product or group in your catalogue. VoiceLine links it automatically and includes it in [Product Analytics](/customer/analytics/product-analytics).
- **Unmatched** — the mention could not be linked to any item in the catalogue. This typically happens when reps use abbreviations, trade names, or shorthand that are not yet registered as aliases.

Unmatched mentions are not lost. They appear in the performance dashboard so admins can review them and link each one to the correct product or group. Linking an unmatched mention saves the term as an alias — all future occurrences are matched automatically.

## Transcription vocabulary

Product names and aliases are automatically added to VoiceLine's custom transcription vocabulary. This means industry-specific terms, brand names, and product codes that a general speech model wouldn't otherwise recognise are picked up correctly from recordings — no extra configuration required.

## Administration interface

After the catalogue is synced or uploaded, the full product hierarchy is visible and navigable in the VoiceLine administration interface. Admins can browse product groups, sub-groups, products, and variants, and manage aliases at any level. The product alias table supports search — type a term to filter entries by product name or any of its aliases.

## Search behaviour

Reps search products by name during visit reports and opportunity creation. Search results include matches against product names and aliases, so reps can find the right item using any known term. Phonetic matching is active — minor pronunciation variations resolve correctly. For large catalogues, VoiceLine prompts the rep to narrow the search by product name, number, or category.
